Trade leagues() are a type of defensive diplomatic relationship that can involve multiple countries.Merchant republics,Veche republics,Venetian republics,Arabian plutocraciesandEastern plutocraciescan form trade leagues and invite other countries to join them; this gives both the leader and the members various diplomatic and trade-related benefits.

### Creating a trade league[edit|edit source]

Countries with an eligible government type can create a trade league by inviting other countries in the diplomatic interface. Only countries with a single province can be invited to be a member of a trade league. The country that created the trade league may have more than one province at the time of formation and may gain more afterwards, but if any of the other members gain additional provinces they will automatically leave the league. If the leader is annexed or changes to a different form of government and a member of the league has an eligibile government form, this country takes over leadership instead. A new leader will also be selected if the current leader drops below−50prestige. If there is no eligible leader, the Trade League disbands. Members can leave a league at any time, excluding Trading Cities.

### Benefits[edit|edit source]

Trade leagues act as defensive alliance between all members, but consume only a single diplomatic relations slot. If the leader embargoes another country, all members automatically embargo that country too without a penalty. If any members in a league gets embargoed, the leader gets a special“Trade League Dispute”casus bellion the offending country.

In addition:

- The trade league leader gets an automatic“Trade Dispute”casus belli on any countries they would be able to generate such a casus belli on via spy action.

### Trading city[edit|edit source]

Countries which are able to create or which already lead a trade league can make a new one-province-minor in one of their provinces through the province view interface, in the same way as aClient State.  The city's national ideas are randomized, but the player can decide upon Trading Cities' name, shield and map color, like when creating acustom nation. This new nation will become independent, and it will inherit the creating nation's state religion, but part of their Trade League.

The first Trading City created in the world has thetagT00, the second T01, and so forth.  Only one Trading City can exist at a time in each Trade Node for a Trade League. If the Trade League is somehow disbanded, or if a player-controlled Trading City leaves it, it will retain its government form until a second province is acquired.

A Trading City can create trade posts and boostsgoods produced in the trade node just like amerchant republic, but cannot lead a Trade League and thus not create other trading cities. A Trading City that gains an additional province will immediately become a Merchant Republic; this most frequently occurs when a Trading City uses a colonist to obtain a second province, or when its separatist rebels enforce their demands in another country.  The latter can occur when a province with the Trading City's culture has separatism, and the Trading City is the only existing nation with its primary culture.

Unlike typical trade league members, a Trading City cannot be kicked out or be diplomatically vassalized by a non-member.  However, there are a few ways a Trading City can be removed from a trade league, intentionally or not.  The simplest way is to switch away from the government reform that allows you to create a trade league (usually Tier 1 Republic reform "Plutocracy"), but this will cause you to leave your trade league.  If fighting in the same war as the Trading City, you can give away its province or transfer its trade power to someone else when intentionally losing a war (or, counterintuitively, when you have reached 100% war score, which forces all demandsor offersto be accepted). As a probably unintended exception, Trading Cities may still leave by becoming a tributary or a trade protectorate, since they are not coded as subjects.

Trade cities start as kingdom government rank by default, unless created in a province in the Holy Roman Empire (which will cause them to start as a duchy.  They cannot change their government type by completing government reforms.

The government type gives the following modifiers, which are similar to those of a standard Merchant Republic:

- +10%Global trade power
- −20Maximum absolutism
- Has the samefactionsand election cycle as a Merchant Republic
