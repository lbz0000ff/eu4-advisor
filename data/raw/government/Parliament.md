Nations with access to theParliamentmechanic can open the Parliament interface via a button on the bottom right of their screen.

Parliamentis a government mechanic activated by the following government reforms :

English Monarchy,British Monarchy,Absolute British Monarchy,Angevin Monarchy,Irish High Kingdom,Scottish Monarchy,Stadhouder Monarchy,Norwegian Monarchy,Great Sejm,Polish Elective Monarchy,Reformed Byzantine Monarchy,Courts of Navarra,Roman Empire,Livonian Constitutional Monarchy*,Livonian States Council

Protectorate Parliament,Constitutional Republic,Parliamentary Republic,American Republic*,Federal Republic*,Dutch Republic,Roman Republic

Certain reforms are mutually exclusive with theParliamentarismreform, e.g.DaimyoandPlutocracy.

*These Reforms disable theNobility estate, as well as the ability to call the diet if enacted.

### Seats[edit|edit source]

Aprovincecan get a seat in the parliament if it

- is acore provinceof the country,
- is part of astate
Each seat in parliament gives the following modifiers:[1]

At any point in time, 20% of the nation's owned cored stated provinces must have a seat in parliament. This value always rounds down but has a minimum of 1. So if a nation has 1 - 9 stated provinces they will need 1 seat in parliament, once they get a 10th province they will need 2. If not enough seats are assigned, the game assigns a seat automatically from time to time.

The modifier"Max possible parliament seats" limits the number of required seats. For example, a nation with theReformed Byzantine Monarchygovernment reform will never need to assign more than 8 seats.

A seat can be assigned manually (with thebutton at the top left of the province window), automatically, or byevents.

However, when a province is assigned a seat,absolutismdecreases by−0.15perprovince development.

### Debates[edit|edit source]

The debate process is as follows:

1. If the country has no ongoing debate, it may start a new debate.
1. The country starts the debate by choosing one issue out of a list of 10[2]randomly selected issues. The amount of issues which can be chosen from can be in- / decreased by the "Number of possible parliament issues" modifier. If none is chosen, the list will reroll on each month tick, but the window has to be reopened to see the new issues.
1. The debate lasts for 5 years ("Parliament debate duration").[3]During this time the country may bribe seats of parliament to pass the debate. Modifiers can change the "Parliament debate duration", but the minimum time is 1 year.
1. Each seat of parliament is assigned a random bribe type to fulfill and has a 10%[4]chance tobackthe motion at the start of the debate. The chance of seats backing the issue at debate start can be in/decreased by a countries"Parliament debate backing chance" which is additive to the default percentage.
1. Each fulfilled bribe increases the chance of the debate succeeding once the 5 year debate period has passed.
1. If the chance of success reaches 100%, the debate immediately ends and succeeds.
1. After 5 years, the debate has a base 10% "Parliament chance of decision" per month.[3]If this happens the debate either fails or succeeds, based on its current success chance.
1. If the debate succeeds, the issue goes into effect for usually 10 years.[3]Some issues have individual effect durations. The modifier "Parliament effect duration" can in- or decrease this time, to a minimum of 1 years.
1. If the debate fails, the country suffers−20prestige,[3]and may begin a new debate.
At any point in time, there must be an active debate or issue, or else the country suffers:[5]

#### Resetting Debates[edit|edit source]

At any point while a debate is still ongoing it can be reset via the Parliament interface. Doing so will end the current debate and allow the nation to immediately start a new one. This will also cause the nation to gain+2Corruption and the modifier "Reset parliamentary debate" for 10 years, which grants+1National Unrest. A debate can only reset once every 20 years.

#### Forcing Issues through[edit|edit source]

TheAbsolute British Monarchygovernment reform enables a decision "Force issue through parliament". This decision will automatically succeed the current debate. It can only be taken once every 20 years.

TheRussian EmpireandGreat Russian Federationgovernment reforms ability "Enforce Absolute Rule" will also immediately succeed the current debate.

#### Bribes[edit|edit source]

In general, most parliament issues will not allow bribes which would be counterproductive to what they want to achieve. For example, a issue which reduces war exhaustion will not allow bribes that would increase war exhaustion.

##### National Bribes[edit|edit source]

Most Bribes have a "National" counterpart. Once a nation has at least 40 seats in their parliament, the normal bribes will be replaced by their "National" counterparts. National Bribes cost 5 times as much as normal bribes, but paying one will cause all seats that also demand this bribe to also start supporting the issue.Note that the "Ignore National Problems" Bribe currently has the same costs as its regular counterpart.Bribes that do not have a "National" equivalent are :

- Grant Local Autonomy
- Appease Reactionaries
- Present a Scapegoat
- Enact Radical Reforms
- Support "vs. side"
- Scale down Militarization (both)
- Act against the Tribes
- Scale down Modernization
- Delay Council Meetings
- Curtail Ruling Power (all)
- Curtail Christians Power
##### Bribe cost[edit|edit source]

The ReformsTwo Treatises of GovernmentandFederal Senate, reduce the cost of Bribes by50%. It has no Effect on Presenting a Scapegoat or placating a estate.Certain Parliament issues have their Bribe costdoubled, these issues are :

- Acts of Impressment
- Acts of High Admiralty
- Maritime Courage Acts
- Act of Naval Professionalism
- Acts of Maritime Professionalism
- Colonial Venture
Bribes which target a nations Government power or "vs. side" cant have their costs doubled. It also has no Effect on Presenting a Scapegoat or placating a estate.

Nations with the government reformsEnglish Monarchy,British Monarchy,Angevin Monarchy,Absolute British Monarchy,Constitutional Republic,Protectorate ParliamentorParliamentary Republichave access to the "English Parliament". Its Bribes have their costs scaled with the Loyalty of a specific Estate.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

- Adopting Parliamentarism before theAge of Absolutism allows the manual assignment of the initial parliament seats. To get enough reform progress, the nation-wide autonomy level must not be too high, which usually means holding few territories and few trade companies.
- Only offer Bribes when the nation can regain the currency being offered quickly or otherwise is unaffected by the consequences; Bribes in the form of ducats, reform progress, or increase of estates' influences are a lot more acceptable than (e.g.) a sharp increase in local autonomy.Note that sometimes, the Bribes available can be to your benefit, such as granting autonomy to a province, allowing you to manually decrease it for Absolutism (at the cost ofunrest), or increasing an Estate's influence beyond one of the thresholds that increases the effectiveness of their bonuses
