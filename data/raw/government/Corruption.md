Corruptionmeasures a country's bureaucratic morality throughout the ranks. A country with high corruption suffers widely from embezzlement and loss of the leader's authority, while a country with no corruption is free to prosper. Unlikeinflation, corruption does not directly affect monetary costs, instead affecting other areas of the state (though it may indirectly affect income due to raising the local autonomy floor of provinces).

### Effects[edit|edit source]

Effects of 100 corruption:[1]

Eliminating corruption enables beneficialCorruption events, while increasing corruption enables increasingly bad events. Corruption also increases the amount ofmonarch powerwhich can be stored at the same level as the respective power cost increase.
