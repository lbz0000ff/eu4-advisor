### Monuments

Leviathan spawns historical monuments spread out across the world. The monuments exist in provinces and are shown above the buildings of a province. Some monuments can be relocated.

Monuments that are active have three possible types of effects: provincial, areal, and global. Provincial modifiers adjust the province the monument is located in; areal modifiers affect all provinces in the local state (including other countries' provinces). Global modifiers affect the owner's country the same way a national idea would. For each monument's modifiers, see the list below.

Many monuments have requirements which their province must fulfill. The monument has no effect if they are not fulfilled and it can't be upgraded.

#### Tiers/Levels

A monument can have 4 tiers/levels:Insignificant,Noteworthy,Significant andMagnificent. If the province which contains a monument is conquered in a war or defects due to rebel occupations, the monument is downgraded one level. The monument doesn't lose a level if the province changes hands peacefully. A monument can be upgraded to increase its level. Upgrading a monument can be done via a button in the monument image in the province window and costs money and takes a long time according to the table below. There are also some events, decisions and missions which upgrade a monument.

The upgrade cost will be changed by−5%for each timeexpand infrastructurehas been used in the province and being bankrupt increases the cost by+50%. The firstZimbabweidea “Stone Masonry” changes the cost by−10%and various missions and events also give discounts to the upgrade cost.

The upgrade time is changed by−1%for eachtax development in the province and the infrastructure idea group eventA Talented Architectfurther changes the time by−10%.

Spending either250 ducats[1]or10000 manpower[2]speeds up the upgrade by 2 years[3]. This can be done multiple times, even in one go, meaning if you have the cash or manpower you can fast-track each upgrade level at once.

#### Relocation

A few monuments can be moved to the capital of their owner. They are marked within thecolumn in the table below. The relocation takes 1-10 days[4]for each unit of distance between the provinces. It costs3 ducats per day[5]. A monument of a non-tributary subject can be moved to the capital of the overlord. Moving a monument doesn't change its requirements, so it will become useless if the new location doesn't fulfill them.

#### Destroying Monuments

Certainmissions andevents can destroy Monuments. A destroyed Monument will still be shown in the province view, but without any Tiers and only if the monuments conditions are fullfilled. A destroyed Monument can be repaired at the cost of1000Ducats. This will instantly restore the monument toinsignificant tier. This cost cannot be reduced.

All Monuments that can be destroyed are:

### Canals

Canalsare late-game 'great projects' that primarily function to ease the movement of fleets between theaters of war. They allow for anyone to pass through, unless they are at war with the country which controls the province in which the canal great project is located. Canals, like monuments, have four levels each with varying bonuses. Each canal provides a provincial bonus of+10/+20/+30/+50trade power and+1%/+2%/+3%/+5%global trade power. 
Canals don't requirelike traditionals 'great projects'.

Only independent nations can build canals. To initiate construction the player must have:

- Level22 administrative technology
- Own specificprovinces or be owned by a subject.
- A large amount ofducats for the cost of the canal, (varies between canals).
There are 3 canals that can be built: the Kiel Canal, the Suez Canal, and the Panama Canal. They cost10,000,20,000, and30,000 respectively and take 10 years. Unlike monuments, their built can't be speed up by spending money or manpower and once built they will be at the highest level, 'magnificent level', already.

During construction,events[6]will occur where the player will have to choose between investingmonarch pointsor wait an additional length oftime before construction can be completed. The duration of this setback is proportional to the total number of days the canal will be under construction from start to finish, so choosing to delay construction will make further setbacks worse, if they occur. Conversely, good events that advance canal construction will lessen the progress-related impact of both good and negative canal events in the future.
