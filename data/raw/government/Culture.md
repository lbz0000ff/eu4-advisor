Culture as represented in EU4 is the representation of the local populaces on a provincial level. The culture represents the combination of the local language, customs, ethnicity and nationality of the people.Religionis split from this list, as it is represented by provincial religion which has a separate representation in the game.

The cultures of EU4 are divided in culture groups consisting of multiple cultures which have similar customs, have a similar language or have a shared history. EU4 has a total of 72 culture groups with multiple different cultures as represented with the two maps to the right.

### Primary culture[edit|edit source]

All countries have one primary culture. This is normally fixed, although it can be changed either through the culture mechanics or via certain national decisions. The main interaction of the nation with local culture is by spendingdiplomacy points.

#### Cultural shift[edit|edit source]

Any accepted culture that has at least 50% of the development of the country’s state cores can be promoted to the primary culture of the nation. This costs100diplomatic power, and replaces the previous ‘Accept Cultural Shift’[1]decision. The previous primary culture will consequently become an accepted culture.

The country cannot shift to a culture that is accepted due to the cultural union granted by Empire level government. Shifting the culture to outside the players cultural union shifts the cultural union, enabling the player to shift back to the preferred culture in the original cultural union.

It can be advantageous for a player to set a new primary culture to be able to form a country with a different tag (with new national ideas), or to be in a larger culture group, to have more brother cultures and save promoted culture slots.

If the nation has very few states active, it can state and de-state provinces to frequently change the primary culture to obtainNationalismcasus belli on more countries, as well as to reduce the time ofcoring.

#### Culture group shift[edit|edit source]

Originally only available via Sinicization, the game has included several ways to switch the groups a certain culture belongs to. Mechanically this is achieved by converting all provinces of the original culture to a new (usually similarly named) culture, which is part of a different group. The following is a list of all cultures that can shift what group they belong to and how:

- Korean to Sino- Korean, switches from Korean to Chinese group. Via the "Sinicize our Culture" decision.
- Oirat, Khalkha, Mongol and Korchin cultures to Sino-Altaic, switches from Altaic to Chinese group. Via formingYuan(can be formed by any Altaic culture nation but shifts only the four mentioned cultures).
- All Tibetan group cultures to Sino-Tibetan, switches from Tibetan to Chinese group. Via the "Sinicize our Culture" decision.
- Vietnamese to Sino- Vietnamese, switches from Mon-Khmer to Chinese group. Via the "Sinicize our Culture" decision.
- Zhuang to Sino- Zhuang, switches from Tai to Chinese group. Via the "Sinicize our Culture" decision.
- Jurchen to Manchu, switches from Evenki to Chinese group. Via formingManchu.
- Georgian to Greco-Georgian, switches from Caucasian to Greek group. ViaGeorgias "Throne of the Romans" orTrebizonds "Georgian Union" mission
- All British group cultures to Anglois, switches from British to French group. Via theAngevin Kingdoms "The Angevin Culture" mission (Shifts only all owned and subjects owned provinces).
- Bosnian, Bulgarian, Byelorussian, Croatian, Czech, Muscovite, Novgorodian, Polish, Ruthenian, Ryazanian, Serbian, Silesian, Slovak, Slovene and Sorbian switch from West Slavic, East Slavic and South Slavic to Slavic group. Via theRussian "Protect the South Slavs" mission (All cultures keep their names in game and are only renamed internally).
- Turkish, Azerbaijani and Turkmeni to Turco-Iranian, switches from Levantine, Iranian and Altaic to Iranian group. Via theAq Qoyunlu/Qara Qoyunlu"Shahanshas of Eranshahr" mission (Shifts only all owned provinces of the three mentioned cultures).
- Gothic switches from Byzantine to Germanic group. Potentially viaTheodoros "A Merchant's Family Home" mission. (Culture keeps its name in game and is only renamed internally).

#### Cultural union[edit|edit source]

Cultural unionswill automatically accept all sub-cultures within their respective culture group. Any nation can become a cultural union of its culture group upon reaching theEmpiregovernment rank, or1000development. As part of this change, it is possible for several nations to be a cultural union for the same culture group. A nation can become an empire as part of a mission, decision, event, reaching a certain development level or by upgrading the government rank; cultural unions make it so that sister cultures don't have to be promoted to be accepted by the country.

### Penalties for unaccepted cultures[edit|edit source]

A province with a non-accepted culture will provide less tax and have higher unrest alongside other penalties. A province with a culture in the same culture group, but not the same culture as the owner will get lighter penalties:

Same culture group(Culture name will be inyellow, and will have a star next to their names on the culture tab)

Different culture group(Culture name will be inred)

Republican Cultural Sufferance

Republicsget an additional cultural sufferance modifier in different culture-group provinces. This stacks with the usual penalty, mitigating but not eliminating it.

### Culture conversion[edit|edit source]

The culture of a province can be changed to another culture if the province is cored by its owner, has the samereligionas its owner (forConfucian, harmonized religions work as well), and has no unrest fromseparatism. The cost for converting culture is10diplomatic powerperdevelopment[2]and the duration for the change to take place is 10 months per development.[3]Like coring costs, culture conversion costs are capped at 30 development, so improving a 30 development province will not increase the cost of converting its culture. Sieges and occupations will pause culture conversion but will not cancel it; the culture conversion will continue as soon as the province is liberated, although conversion will be cancelled if the province's ownership changes. If the conversion is cancelled before progress reaches 10%, all diplomatic points will be refunded.

A province can have its culture changed to the primary culture of the province's owner. WithThe Cossacks, a province's culture can also be changed to any owned neighbouring province's culture or its culture can be restored to whatever it was originally.

Converting a province from culture A to culture B will incur a relations hit with every country whose primary culture is culture A.

### Primary nation[edit|edit source]

Most cultures have aprimary nation. These nation will keep uncontestedcoresindefinitely[8], so long as their cores are in provinces of their culture - culture conversion will force these cores to obey the normal 50/150 year expiry timers for cores depending on whether or not the new culture is the same culture group as the nation with a core there. Additionally the primary nation is the default separatists target of a province if no other valid target exist even with no core on it. Primary nations of each culture can be found in the list below.

### Lost cultures[edit|edit source]

Cultures in theLost Culturesgroup do not exist at game start, but can be created as custom nations or during theinvasion.  The only Lost Culture that can be re-discovered is the Roman culture byRestoring the Roman Empirethat changes the primary culture of the nation. All provinces with a culture in the culture group of the former nation's primary culture will change to Roman culture. However you can change to any Lost Culture usingconsole commands, by typing in the console "change_culture_court ruler [Culture]" then typing "event culture_religion_events.22" this will fire an event with one of the options being changing the nation's primary culture to the ruler's culture.

Other cultures that do not exist at game start can sometimes be created by event, such as theReturn of the Norse culture,emergent colonial culturesin Colonial Nations for American, Mexican, and Brazilian culture, and Anglois for those who restore theAngevin Kingdom. However, these are not in the Lost Cultures culture group (though Norse was formerly in it)

### Culture groups[edit|edit source]

Note that culture groups are not just based on linguistic criteria - for example, Romanian is grouped with the Carpathian cultures due to cultural similarities and for gameplay purposes, even though Carpathian is not a linguistic family.

Primary nations of each culture are listed to the right of each culture.

WithDomination there exists a additional culture group - Slavic. It can be founded via the Russian missions and combines all cultures of the East Slavic, West Slavic and South Slavic groups, except albanian, into one group.
