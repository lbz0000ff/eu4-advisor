Each country is headed by aruler. In EU4, rulers are influential in the running of a country, having between 0 and 6 points ofadministrative, diplomatic and military skill;  these skills contribute directly to the nation's monthly generation of monarch power. Rulers may be either amonarch, who as an heir inherits the title from a relative in amonarchy, or an elected leader of arepublicortheocracy, chosen by the AI or player at regular intervals.

### Personalities[edit|edit source]

Rulers of AI nations will have personalities that determine how they act. The particular personality assigned to each ruler is strongly correlated with the category of their highest skill, with administrative, diplomatic, and military skill leading to administrator, diplomat, and militaristic personalities respectively. Knowledge of this can help players predict the personality of an upcoming heir.
Every single ruler/heir have traits that develop over time. These personalities grant a modifier for the nation as well as changes to the AI's actions. All personalities unlock new options in some events, as well as have events of their own.

### Monarchy[edit|edit source]

Monarchs (rulers of countries with amonarchygovernment type) rule until death, and have an heir to succeed them and inherit the throne.

#### Dynasty[edit|edit source]

A dynasty is acquired through:

- Spread from other countries
- Pretender rebels
- EventsDynamic “disasters”, such as theWar of the Rosesor theCastilian Civil War, or generic events, such as some of theDynastic eventsand particularly those that may happen when a country is heirless
- Dynamic “disasters”, such as theWar of the Rosesor theCastilian Civil War, or generic events, such as some of theDynastic eventsand particularly those that may happen when a country is heirless
- a local dynasty when a ruler dies without an heir
- the dynasty of a (consort-) regent when the heir dies during a regency
Most countries at game start, in 1444 or in other bookmarks, already have a dynasty. There are some exceptions to this, notably Poland in 1444 which is in the special situation ofinterregnum.

##### Share and spread[edit|edit source]

Dynasties, especially those initially found in powerful countries, can be shared by several countries. A shared dynasty grants a +25 bilateral bonus torelationswith all countries with which the dynasty is shared.

Spreading your dynasty involves first and foremostroyal marriages,prestigeand several other parameters, which, as of now, can only be guessed through in-game empirical evidence.

##### Succession and inheritance[edit|edit source]

A shared dynasty is one of the requirements for theclaim thronemechanic, which is an important part of thesuccession warandinheritancemechanics.[1]

#### Royal marriage[edit|edit source]

Countries ruled by monarchs may arrange royal marriages with others.

A royal marriage is one of the requirements for theclaim thronemechanic, while being an important part of thesuccession warandinheritancemechanics.

Each royal marriage gives 0.1legitimacyper year, but entering into a marriage with a nation with lower prestige causes an immediate drop of 1-5 legitimacy.

##### Consort[edit|edit source]

A royal marriage has a chance to produce a consort to the nation's ruler (queen-consort for male rulers or prince-consort for female rulers). The consort will belong to the other nation's dynasty. There is also a chance of getting a consort of a local minor noble dynasty if the nation gains an heir without a consort present. Having a consort will give a slight relations boost with the nation of origin.

The consort will take the role of the de facto ruler in a time of regency - thus lifting the restrictions placed by the regency rule - until the designated heir comes of age. It is possible for the consort to remain in power after the heir becomes old enough through events or in case of death.

#### Heir[edit|edit source]

A monarch has an heir, who may represent a son, daughter, cousin or other relative. In amonarchy, an heir has an associated claim strength (from 0 to 100), which determines their startinglegitimacyupon succeeding the throne. Heirs come of ruling age at 15, aregencywill otherwise rule until they come of age.

In cases where no heir exists, the mention "No Legal Heir" will stand instead of the heir's name, age and claim strength. If at ruler's death, there is still no heir, the country may:

- Be at the receiving end of a dynasty spread from another country it has a royal marriage with
- Become the junior partner of apersonal union
- Face asuccession warat the issue of which it becomes the junior partner of a personal union with the winning country.
- Get a new ruler from the deceased monarch's dynasty
- Get a new ruler from a randomly-chosen dynasty among the ones figuring in the game files for the country ("A XYZ noble will accede to the throne")
- Be inherited directly (Seeinheritance)
The determination of which scenario is to happen rests on several factors, such as, among others, royal marriages, dynasty, rivals, prestige, legitimacy, military strength and totaldevelopment.

##### Name[edit|edit source]

The player can choose the name of a new heir or re-roll another random name.

##### Heir claim[edit|edit source]

Heir claim represents the legitimacy of the heir to the throne in the eyes of the people, and is a number ranging from 0–100 that is assigned when an heir is generated. Upon ascending to the throne, a ruler's legitimacy will be set to the value of their heir claim. In game, heir claim will sometimes only be referred to asStrong,Average, orWeak, which gives a rough idea of the value. Claim strengths of 0–33.3 are counted as weak, 33.4–66.6 is a average claim and 66.7+ is a strong claim. Having a lower heir claim (average or weak) gives a higher chance of pretender rebels arising.

The formula for heir chance appears to be random, but weighted to give higher values, making the majority of heirs have a strong claim.

If theLeviathan DLC is active, heirs will usually start with an average claim, but their claim strength changes every month. Usually it increases, but some modifiers can make it decrease.

Monthly change is calculated as follows:

- Base value:+0.20(says +0.21, but is actually less)
- Prestige:−0.05to+0.05at −100 / +100 prestige
- Corruption:+0.03at 100 corruption
Other ways to change the claim strength are taking the "Support Ruling Dynasty"parliamentary debate, which increases the claim strength by+10; two events forMing; andonefrom theArt of Warbuilt-in DLC.

##### Introduce new heir[edit|edit source]

If a monarchy has no heir, it can choose to introduce a new heir at a cost of−20legitimacyand−20prestige, so long as the country meets the following requirements:

- Not in a regency
- Not at war
- Positive prestige
- At least 90 legitimacy
- Does not have a fixed dynasty
An heir introduced in this way will come from a local dynasty that is different from the country's current ruling monarch and will have a weak claim to the throne.

Further, introducing a new heir will grant a 20-yearRestoration of Unioncasus belli to any country with whom the monarchy has a royal marriage. Allies with a Restoration of Union casus belli may becomeDomineeringand will tend to break any alliance with the monarchy.

##### Disinherit[edit|edit source]

A country may disinherit an heir if so is wished, at a cost of−50prestige. After this action is done the country won't have any legal heir, and will be at risk of falling under aPersonal Union, just like any other country without a legal heir (It is not possible to "return" to the disinherited heir).

##### Regency[edit|edit source]

A country whose ruler is a child (usually because he or she was still a child when the previous ruler died) will be ruled by aregency council, using its skills instead of the ruler's, until the ruler comes of age at 15 years.[2]The regency will be provided by the estate with the highest influence in your country, ifLeviathan is active. While in regency, a country cannot declare war unless following theNahuatlreligion (but can still be called to war or be attacked). Additionally, while in regency, the country gets the following modifier:

If a consort is present, they will take the reins over the nation until the designated heir comes of age, effectively lifting the restrictions of placed by the regency rule; in particular, a country in a consort regency can declare war.

The legitimacy of the consort regency when taking power will depend on the legitimacy of the late ruler and the current claim strength of the heir, whichever is higher.

If theLeviathan DLC is active, it is possible to extend the regency for the heir by 5 years, at the cost of10legitimacy. This might be desirable to further grow his claim before he takes power, or if the consort has much better skills.

Should the consort regent die during the regency, an estate-led regency council will take power (see above). This regent cannot die. An exception is if the regency has been extended and the heir is of age, in which case the regency simply ends.

Should the heir die during a consort regency, the consort will take power one day later, with a legitimacy of 75.

Should the heir die during an estate-led regency, an 18 year old0/0/0monarch with a random local dynasty takes power without changing legitimacy.

##### Interregnum[edit|edit source]

An interregnum is effectively anarchy; no leadership is in control of the country. This does not happen during regular gameplay. It is possible to get one for example by becoming a kingdom while being a Pirate Republic with a Mutineer council in power. Interregnums generally do not last very long; a year is probably average, though they can last much longer. When the interregnum ends, a new leader will be chosen based on the same parameters as if the leader had just died, e.g. a dynasty from a royal marriage, or a new dynasty from the nobility of the country.

Nearby kingdoms in interregnum can be easy to take advantage of. If the player arranges a royal marriage, the new king will likely be a part of the player's dynasty. They will also have no heir. An heirless dynasty with a royal marriage can beClaimed, giving a CB to form a Personal Union. Declaring war against a country one is married into causes stability loss, but this is a small price to pay.
It is possible for this tactic to fail, as other nations could form royal marriages as well. A high prestige will ensure that one's dynasty prevails.

In 1444,PolandandHungarystart this way due to their joint monarch having died without issue in the Battle of Varna, andBohemiaalso, because its lords did not recognize its previous ruler's posthumous son (Ladislaus the Posthumous) as legitimate. Hungary is scripted to get a specific dynasty (as Ladislaus gets accepted there, and he is a Habsburg), and Poland similarly gets a choice between a PU withLithuaniaor a local noble's dynasty. Bohemia does have possible events but there is still the possibility to get the player's dynasty. As such, nearby countries strong enough to take on Bohemia, such asAustria, can get a big boost for the early years if they choose to do so.

#### Deaths[edit|edit source]

When a monarch dies, the country takes a−1stability hit. When a monarch leads an army in a battle or a siege, this results in a−2stability hit instead. Heirs that die either in battle or while sieging will result in a−1stability hit, while dying while not leading any units will not result in any stability hit, although there are certain events that may fire when an heir dies that can cause a stability hit.

### Republic[edit|edit source]

In mostrepublics, a group of people hold power over a country until the next election cycle, the length of which depending on the type of republic that is in control. In cases where there are no elections, rulers appear almost identically to those of a monarchy, though they lack a persistent dynasty. Republics do not have heirs, and have the associatedrepublican traditionmechanic, rather thanlegitimacy. Republics are unable to obtainroyal marriages, exept for some special cases.

#### Election[edit|edit source]

Elections take place at different times for different types of republics. The standart election cycle is 4 years. Various government reforms can shorten or lengthen the government cycle and some reforms don't have a cycle. In that case the election only happens when the current ruler dies. See the "Republics" page for the exact term length of all tier 1 reforms.

Some reforms can additionally change the length of the election cycle. These reforms are:

- Shortening the election cycle by1year:Frequent Elections,New Men,A Revolutionary Council
- Lengthening the election cycle by1year:Consolidation of Power,Absolute Power of the President
The Court tab will always show when the next election will be, right below the current ruler.

Once the current rulers term is up or if they die, the "Election!" event will fire. This event will offer the election of one of three candidates, each inclined towards one particular skill: administrative, diplomatic, or military. The ruler will have a4skill in that particular one, and1in both the others.

Additionally, the "Force Re-election" decision, which is enabled by theFormalize the Right of Re-electionandLegislative Assemblygovernment reforms, can at the cost of5republican tradition immediately fire the "Election!" event.

The government reformsItalian SignoriaandPolitical Dynastiesenable an additional option in the Election event: to elect a relative of the previous ruler. This candidate will have random skills if the nation is anItalian Signoria, or random skills which will all be reduced by2each if the nation hasPolitical Dynasties. Electing this candidate costsrepublican tradition equal to1times the length of the election cycle.

##### Reelection[edit|edit source]

Alternatively, if the election wasn't triggered by the ruler dying, the former ruler can be reelected. This will upgrade the ruler's skills by1each and keep them for another term; the nation will also gain50power in one category.

Reelections aren't free: to balance out the skill gain,republican tradition will be reduced harshly.

Therepublican tradition cost for re-election is tied to the length of the election cycle:2.5republican tradition per year of ruling (e.g. a 4-year election cycle costs10republican tradition, an 8-year election cycle costs20).

If a ruler is re-elected during an election that was triggered by the "Force Re-election" decision, the cost of re-electing them will be increased by50%.

Re-electing a ruler while below20republican tradition will turn the nation into a Dictatorship. Should the dictator die whilerepublican tradition remains below50, the nation will become a monarchy. Some special republics can't fall under a dictatorship.

##### Changing the effect of elections[edit|edit source]

With elections being the main feature of republics, various government reforms interact with the mechanic in many different ways. The following is a list of all reforms that add some small additional mechanic to elections:

- Re-elections reduce the Influence of all Estates by−10%:Consolidation of Power
- Elections raise the influence of the candidate's faction by20:Plutocracy,Venetian Government,Veche Republic,Trading City,Pirate Republic,Trade Company,National Assembly
- Elections raise the loyalty of the candidates estate by+10:Supremacy of the Diet
- Rulers can be re-elected only once but give unique bonuses while they rule:Civic Republicanism
#### Death[edit|edit source]

Besides triggering an election, a republican ruler's death does not cause a stability loss, except if they die in combat or while leading a siege, which causes a−1stability hit.

Theocracies are states ruled by a religious head, and are subject to thedevotionmechanic in place oflegitimacy. Theocracies are unable to obtainroyal marriageswith other nations, reducing their diplomatic abilities somewhat.

### Tribes[edit|edit source]

For all intents and purposes, atribe works exactly the same as a monarchy.

Like in monarchies, if the ruler dies without a heir, a random new ruler assumes the throne.

#### Tribal succession crisis[edit|edit source]

Steppe NomadsandTribal Federationscan get succession crises when their rulers die, depending on their current heir's age and the nation'shorde unity/legitimacy.

If horde unity was below 50 when a new ruler assumes the throne (be it the previous heir or a random new ruler), the nation will get the "Tribal succession crisis" modifier, which causes+10national unrest until ruler death or until all pretender rebels are killed.

Tribal Federationsdo not have horde unity. For them to trigger this crisis, the heir assuming the throne must have had a claim of below 50. Additionally, a ruler dying without an heir will always trigger the crisis for them.

Additionally,Steppe NomadsandTribal Federationscan't usually get regencies. Should the ruler die while the heir is underage, the heir will be killed via an event and a random new ruler assumes the throne. If horde unity was below 50 when this happened, the event will have the option to side with the heir. Siding with the heir will establish a regency and give the "Tribal succession crisis" modifier. Granting thetribes estate privilegeSecured Successionwill prevent this if the heir has a total skill of at least 12 monarch points.

Tribal Federationscan only side with the heir if they get aconsort regencywith less than 50 legitimacy.

### Native tribes[edit|edit source]

Anative tribeuses nolegitimacy or any of its equivalents. They also have no heirs or consorts; if the ruler dies, a new one is created randomly.

With these reforms, the nation will have two internal factions fighting over the power in the nation. One faction will be more authoritarian (usually monarchist) and the other will be more "democratic". The nation will haveelectionsat different times, depending on what faction is in power. If the "democratic" faction is in power there will be an election 4 years after the current ruler was elected. If the authoritarian faction is in power, elections will only be held when the current ruler dies.

Every election will allow the choice between only two candidates, one of each faction. These candidates have random skills and a claim of95.

To see what faction is currently in power, the nation has a scale that goes from−100to100. While the scale is at0or negative, the "democratic" faction will be in power; while it is positive, the authoritarian faction will be in power. Electing a candidate will swing the balance by33towards the candidate's faction. Various events and other actions can also swing the balance of power.

Additionally, each faction will grant the nation a set of modifiers while they are in power. See the various reforms for details.

#### Monarchy specific special successions[edit|edit source]

Special successions for monarchies.

##### Harem[edit|edit source]

Nations with theOttoman Government,Modernized Ottoman GovernmentorSultanate of Rûmreforms have access to the Harem succession. Within 6 months of the ruler turning 30, aneventwill fire, which allows the nation to choose one of three heirs. These heirs will always be 10 years old.

The first will either have their military skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their administrative skill,orhave their military skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their diplomatic skill,orhave random skills,orhave all of their skills increased by+1(1% chance).

The second will either have their administrative skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their military skill,orhave their administrative skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their diplomatic skill,orhave random skills.

The third will either have their diplomatic skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their military skill,orhave their diplomatic skill increased by+1but a maximum of5in their administrative skill,orhave random skills.

Should at any point the current ruler die without an heir, a randomly generated ruler that is 25 years old will come to power. This ruler cannot have more than3points in any skill.

If the harem was separated from the state during the "Reign of Women" event chain, heirs which would have a maximum to one of their skills will have have that maximum reduced from 5 to 3.

##### Secured Succession[edit|edit source]

Nations with theHermit Kingdomreform have access to a "secured succession". Within 6 months of the ruler turning 40, aeventwill fire, which allows the nation to choose one of three heirs. These heirs will always be 10 years old, have random skills and a random claim strength.

Should at any point the current ruler die without an heir, a completely randomly generated ruler will come to power.

##### Polish election[edit|edit source]

Nations with thePolish Elective Monarchy,Livonian Elective MonarchyorElective Monarchy(for custom nations) government reforms have a type of succession called "Polish Election". Instead of a fixed heir, they will have a ranked list of available heirs, out of which the one with the most support will become the next king upon ruler death. This list always includes a local heir, with a base support of10. The elective monarchy can spend10prestige to increase their heir's support by5.

Additionally to the local heir, every nation that is of the same religion as the elective monarchy can assign adiplomat to "Support Heir", which increases support for their own candidate. Once this has been done, the supported heir will permanently become part of the current list. While the nation keeps adiplomat supporting their heir, that candidate has a monthly chance of5%×the nation's diplomatic reputation to increase their support by1.

Should a foreign heir become ruler, that nation will gain24times the elected monarch's skills as monarch power,25prestige and10legitimacy. Should the local heir win, nobody gains anything.

Any country can see the current ranking of candidates, as well as their age and monarch skills, by hovering over the current heir's name.

Elective monarchies cannot fall under a personal union, but they can claim other nations' thrones, which is exceedingly easy since they usually have access to many different dynasties.

Additionally, elective monarchies can get a variety ofevents, based on whether the local nobles like the elected heir.

##### Kalmar Succession[edit|edit source]

Nations with theKalmar Uniongovernment have access to the "Kalmar Succession" mechanic. Whenever a new Ruler ascends the throne of the Union leader, anevent chainwill start.

The union leader then has the choice between trusting that their subjects will also elect the new ruler, or grant them either small or big concessions to sway them to do so.

Small concessions will reduce the union leaders nobility loyalty by10and give the "Privileges for the Union's Nobility" modifier until the next election, which grants:

- −10%National tax modifier
- −10%Vassal force limit contribution
All subjects that have at least50liberty desire will gain15nobility loyalty as well as the "Granted Noble Privileges" modifier until the next election, which grants:

- +10%National tax modifier
- +10%Nobility loyalty equilibrium
- +10%Nobles influence
- −5%Liberty desire
Big concessions are only available if at least one Scandinavian subject has at least75%liberty desire. They will reduce the union leader's Nobility loyalty by15and their legitimacy by10and give the "Extensive Privileges for the Union's Nobility" modifier until the next election, which grants:

- −25%National tax modifier
- −20%Vassal force limit contribution
All subjects that have at least50liberty desire will gain25nobility loyalty as well as the "Granted Extensive Noble Privileges" modifier until the next election, which grants:

- +25%National tax modifier
- +20%Nobility loyalty equilibrium
- +20%Nobles influence
- −15%Liberty desire
The subjects will then get an event to either accept or reject the new king.

If a subject accepts the king, their overlord will gain10prestige and5legitimacy. Both subject and overlord will also gain the opinion modifier “Elected the same King” (+25opinion /−1yearly decay) towards each other.

If a subject rejects the king, their overlord will lose20prestige, the subject will gain20%liberty desire and asize 3 andsize 2 Pretender revolt will rise up in the subject's capital area.

If the union leader has Norway or Sweden as their subject as well as a second subject in the Scandinavia region which has less development than Sweden/Norway, Sweden and/or Norway will have the third option to instead of accepting/rejecting the king, suggest their own regent to rule the union. They can then choose between a administrative, diplomatic or military regent.

The regent will have+2in their respective skill and−1in both others. They will also have a maximum skill of 5 in their respective skill and a maximum of 3 in both others. They will have a claim strength of40and will be between 30 and 50 years old.

The union leader can then either accept or reject this alternative regent.

If the regent is rejected, the subject that suggested them will gain20liberty desire and the overlord will lose10prestige. The subject will also get asize 1 revolt (type depends on the chosen regent).

If the regent is accepted and the union has more members than the one which proposed the regent, a re-election happens. The union leader again has the choice of granting concession (see above) and the subjects again have the choice between accepting or rejecting the new king (see above), but this time can't suggest a alternative regent. Subjects will never reject a ruler they suggested.

The choices the AI will make during this event chain depend heavily on their liberty desire (if they are the subject). Good rulers (14+ skills) are also extremely unlikely to be rejected.

##### Foreign slave rulers[edit|edit source]

Nations with theMamluk Governmentdo not have heirs. Instead aneventfires whenever the current ruler dies. This event will allow the selection of various different rulers. There will always be aforeign candidate, but everyaccepted culture will have the chance to add a candidate to the event, up to a maximum of 4 candidates. The candidates all have random skills and their skills are hidden. They will all be between 30 and 50 years old.

If theIntegrated Ottoman Officialsgovernment reform is enacted, the ruler skills won't be hidden during the selection.

The claim of the candidate depends on how big theirculture is in the nation. The calculation is:100− the amount ofstated development that culture has. So, a Circassian candidate will have a claim of100, if the nation owns no statedCircassian land. Should for example an Egyptian candidate be elected while the nation owns only Egyptian land, they will have a claim of0.

Additionally, electing the foreign candidate will grant the nation+15army tradition.

The foreign candidate will usually be ofCircassian culture.

Should all Circassian provinces be owned by the nation or nations that are at war with or rivalled to it, or should Circassian be an accepted culture in the nation and is randomly selected as regular candidate in the succession event, the special Circassian foreign candidate will not be available. Instead the foreign candidate will be of a culture that has a province producingSlaves which is owned by a Muslim nation that is not at war with or rivaled to the Mamluks.

##### Lottery election[edit|edit source]

Nations with theVenetian GovernmentorSortitiongovernment reform have access to lottery elections instead of regular Elections. Their elections only happen when the current ruler dies. The election event will have three candidates to choose from, like regular elections. Reelections are not possible. The three candidates will all have their respective skill increased by2and the other two skills reduced by1. However, bothVenetian GovernmentandSortitionincrease all skills of the elected ruler by+1, meaning that effectively the three candidates have random skills with their respective skill being increased by+3. The skills of the candidates are shown in the election event, but do not already take into account the+1to all skills. The candidates will all be aged between 50 and 75 years. To somewhat balance this out, bothVenetian GovernmentandSortitionincrease the average monarch lifespan by+25%.

The player may (at the cost of25republican tradition) choose which candidate to elect. Alternatively they can let the lottery decide. See the regularElectionssection above for information how the different candidates can influence the nation further.

If the nation has theItalian SignoriaorPolitical Dynastiesgovernment reform in addition to theSortitionreform (Venetian Government is mutually exclusive with both), a fourth candidate will be available during the election. This candidate will be between the ages of 30 and 50 and will have random skills if the nation hasPolitical Dynasties, or random skills but all increased by+1if the nation hasItalian Signoria(this is becausePolitical Dynastiesreduces all their skills by1butSortitionincreases all skills by+1after election). Electing them costs only15republican tradition.

##### Consolidate Power in the Doge[edit|edit source]

A nation with thePlutocracy,Veche Republic, orGreat Veche Republicgovernment reform that enacts theConsolidate Power in the Dogereform loses their access to elections. Instead whenever their current ruler dies Stability is reduced by1and a new one is generated completely randomly.

##### Pirate elections[edit|edit source]

Pirate electionswork mostly the same as the elections of normal republics, besides the fact that electing a candidate will increase the influence of that candidates faction by 20. However they have one important additional feature: legendary pirates. A ruler with theLegendary Pirate trait can be reelected at no cost torepublican tradition. Additionally, depending on the location of the republic's capital region and the date of the game, special historical legendary pirates will be available in the election event. Electing a historical pirate for the first time will also usually gift the nation aheavy ship, named after that pirate's flagship (this ship will be a flagship if the nation does not already have one).
