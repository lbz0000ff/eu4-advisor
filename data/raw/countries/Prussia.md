Prussiais aformable countryin Europe that can be formed byBrandenburg, theTeutonic Order, or any country withPrussian, Saxon, or Pomeranian culture (besides end-game tags such asGermanyand a unified, centralisedHoly Roman Empire). It also exists in campaigns started in 1525 or later. Prussia is a powerful military state with strongmorale,disciplineandmilitary tradition; enough to ensure stronggeneralsin commanding large armies, well-trained troops in aforementioned armies and good military rulers.

If played correctly, Prussia can dominate Central Europe and the rest of theHRE. Prussia is considered one of the more advanced nations due to the difficult requirements and challenges that must be overcome to form, therefore it is not recommended forbeginners. However, once inner quarrels are settled and the homeland is secured it can easily become one of the most entertaining yet still challenging nations, that can play a decisive role in theLeague Wars.

### Formation[edit|edit source]

There are three possible decisions that form Prussia. One is for theTeutonic Order, and is based on the secularization of that order into the Duchy of Prussia in 1525. One is forBrandenburg, based on the Electors of Brandenburg, having inherited the Duchy of Prussia, crowning themselves King in Prussia in 1701. The last is for any other country with Prussian, Saxon or Pomeranian culture, representing another German country taking the path that Brandenburg did historically. Note that the latter two decisions will elevate the nation to kingdomrankand will consequently make it leave theHoly Roman Empireunless it is an elector.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

Getting the key provinces

The classic way to form Prussia is by starting as Brandenberg. Build up your military, get a few strong allies, and wait for The Fate of Neumark event. Purchase the required provinces for 100 gold, then immediately declare war upon Pomerania. At the same time, start to form a spy network in the Teutonic Order, rivaling them if need be. If all goes well, you should steamroll Pomerania and can complete the next mission (potentially declaring war on Stettin to get the required number of provinces too). This will give you claims on many of the Teutonic Order's best lands. Gather your allies and declare war, taking the required provinces to form Prussia (Königsberg and Danzig or Stolp).

Alternatively, you can also start as the Teutonic Order. This route involves much less warring but relies on your ability to do two things: 1) Prevent The Prussian Confederation disaster and 2) Defeat or stave off Poland-Lithuania. The Teutonic order generally cannot defeat Poland-Lithuania in a one-on-one war, and if the Prussian Confederation fires, you will be forced into a war in which you are the attacker, dragging you into a war with Poland without many of your allies. The best way to do this is to join the Holy Roman Empire as quickly as possible; you will typically be set back by an inability to declare war, but this will help you to avoid a losing war with Poland and will help your eventual expansion.

The trick to doing either of these options is that you must be quick; ideally this process takes no longer than about 10-15 in-game years. The Teutonic Order is usually attacked by Poland-Lithuania early on, and starts the game facing down The Prussian Conferederation disaster, which will cause half of their country to secede; afterwards, this new country, and Poland-Lithuania, will typically fully annex the rest of the Order, meaning that you will either perish or need to fight a much larger country to become Prussia. If you can take the key provinces before this occurs, you will be in much better shape.

Converting to ProtestantismThe next step is to convert to Protestantism. This isn't very hard; the only real decision to be made is whether you want to convert right away to gain a center of reformation or whether you would like to wait for awhile for events and other centers to convert your provinces. Be advised that converting early on may make you more of a target for aggression by Catholic countries.

Final Considerations Before FormingBefore forming Prussia, make sure that your economy is in very good shape. Prussia suffers from a massive -50% governing capacity, often putting the forming country well over governing capacity, which significantly increases many costs; it also decreases your ability to use the Prussian Militarism mechanic. Accepting new ideas and traditions (a good idea) may also result in a loss of traditions that increase income. Pre-emptively adopting Administrative ideas, or even Economic ideas, may be a good idea.

How to PrussiaOnce Prussia is formed, you can generally begin to expand quickly. Prussia is capable of taking on just about any nation of the same size and win in a one-on-one duel, especially with investment into Prussian militarism and a drilled army. Prussia will also begin to swim in military points thanks to each Prussian monarch having a +3 bonus to their military stat (making a greater than 50% chance of having a 6 military monarch at any given time). Invest this in provinces, technology, and ideas to become a small but mighty force with great generals. Prussian traditions also give a reduction to aggressive expansion, and furthermore, attacking countries of one religion (e.g., Catholic) will cause reduced aggressive expansion among now-Protestant/Reformed other countries, making coalitions less of a threat.

At this point, begin to prepare for the League War. Attack Catholic countries and convert their lands. Force-convert smaller HRE countries to Protestant. Begin to form alliances with the Emperor's rivals, especially France or the Ottomans if possible. Drill your armies, amass a treasury, and cause as much havoc for the Emperor as you can. If you can form a Protestant League with sufficient manpower, you can overthrow the Emperor, become emperor of a now-Protestant HRE, and from that point you are good to go!
