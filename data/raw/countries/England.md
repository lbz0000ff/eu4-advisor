Ambition:

With the treaty of Tours in May, the English Lancasters and French Valois families have seemingly traded territory for peace. Among its stipulation are the handover of the county of Maine, a point that was never cleared with the English Parliament, and which is likely to anger noble and commoner alike on the far side of the channel. The fear of public backlash has so far lead to this handover being stalled for months, with the word of the English negotiators increasingly being questioned in the French court.

England is itself in a precarious situation. A king since he was nine months old, Henry VI has now grown up to assume the crown and actual rule of the kingdom. Even as an adult however Henry seems unwilling to rule, some would say he is even worryingly detached from reality. Intriguing nobles of the realm have instead come to dominate Henry's regime, assuming power and positioning themselves for a future succession. Such feuds might well cripple the internal stability of the Kingdom, especially if hostilities in France commence again.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

England is a large country in a relatively unique position geographically, mechanically and economically as sea tiles separate theBritish Islesfrom continental Europe and it controls a significant portion of the English Channel endtrade node.

Defensively, the sea tiles separating theBritish Islesrequire an invader to utilize transports and associated escorts to launch any successful invasion. Few nations early in the game are able to support the combined army and naval costs required to successfully invade the British Isles and defend from opportunistic continental neighbors. Offensively, the sea tiles reduce theaggressive expansionexperienced by continental Europe during the consolidation of the British Isles under English rule. Economically, the endtrade nodeof the English Channel provides significant income as the game progresses enabling a strong income to support peaceful colonial or military expansion.

England has a uniquegovernmentnamed theEnglish Monarchyand suffers the historical political crisis ofWar of the Rosesfomenting during an interlude of the long-lastingHundred Years WaragainstFrance. TheEnglish Monarchyhas access toParliamentwhich can vote on issues that provide special bonuses to the kingdom. However, Parliament requires seatedprovincesto be bribed one way or another to pass issues, which can be a severe hindrance to the monarch. TheWar of the Rosesmay determine whether or not England is able to resolve theHundred Years Warover traditionally English territory in the continental Normandy, Gascony and Vasconia areas (borderingFrance).

Startup:England starts the 1444 bookmark possessing 40provinces, 338 totaldevelopment, and is considered agreat power. The King of England isHenry VI of House Lancasterwhoseskillsare0 Adm/0 Dip/0 Mil.England begins the game allied toPortugal. Besides itsprimary culture, England also accepts Gascon and Normancultures. The English Parliament is composed of 5 seats represented by London, Cambridge, Kent, Lancashire and Oxford provinces.

England starts in a very defensible position. Because of the sea separating the island from mainland Europe, the general inability of the AI to handle navies and England's high base development, they are in a favorable and well-defended starting position. After the Wars of the Roses disaster and the Lollard Heresy event end, England is in a good spot to expand into the British islands, mainland Europe, or the many colonial areas. Additionally, the early-game "Surrender of Maine" event will almost certainly allow it to declare a Restoration of Union war on France. While this event may be used to get an early personal union over France without relying on the mission to do so, the war will be very difficult. England will be able to call in very few allies because of the short available time to build favors, and the Wars of Roses disaster will add an additional challenge to defeating France. The lollardy heresy can be avoided by the option to tolerate them, and I advise you should, since it will give you +1 tolerance of heretics. Papal influence may be useful in the short term in order to build stability after the Wars of the Roses and to provide an early boost to the country, but if the player plans on switching toAnglican orProtestant then it is not worth relying on in the long term, as the opportunity to switch appears roughly 100 years after game start.

#### Forming Great Britain and Unifying the Isles[edit|edit source]

The British Isles (outside of England) comprises ofScotland(and their vassal inThe Isles), as well as several Irish minor nations such asLeinsterandDesmondwhich must be dealt with to secure the island (the Scottish lands are required to form Great Britain but the Irish lands are not) The startinggeneral of John Talbot is well suited for these conquests given that he starts withtwo fire,four shock andthree maneuver pips, which is generally higher than those that England can recruit at the start of the game. The missionRaise an Armygrants permanent claims on the entire British Isles, alongside asubjugation casus belli on Scotland (lasting 25 years), which is the catalyst for the conquest of the remainder of the Isles.

You can createvassals in all Irish nations, however directly conquering them may be preferable for the reason of making instant gains in the region. If the English wish, they may releaseMeathor vassalise another Irish minor to grant them the entirety of Ireland. This can be advantageous due to theforce limit contributions frommarch subjects. Due to the player havingclaims over the entirety of the British Isles, there is no diplomatic cost to conquering Ireland even if they do not declare the allies of the Irish minors as co-belligerents, provided that they are taken directly.

Following this, the Crown of Ireland Act can be unlocked by completing theConquer Irelandmission. This Act once passed either releasesIrelandas apersonal union state (with no diplomatic relations cost), or can provide a bonus tounrest reduction,development cost, and monthlyautonomy reduction in the Irish areas. This can be a unique alternative to holding Ireland under a march, however a personal union can be randomly integrated based on thediplomatic reputation of the player. If the player wishes to keep a permanent vassal, they may wish to simply vassalise the Irish and declare them a march.

##### Subjugating Scotland[edit|edit source]

It would be recommended for the player to not declare Scotland as arival, as it will hinder their attempts to turn Scotland into a loyal subject. Conquering Scotland can be tricky due to them either beingguaranteed by or becomingallies with {[flag|France}}. You do not need to fight the French immediately however, as waiting may lead to the French being unwilling to join, breaking the alliance, or being able to directly combat the french. There are multiple ways that the French can be dealt with or ignored.

Ignoring France is the first strategy, which is where the player ignores the French lands and occupies Scotland, waiting for the Scottish to capitulate whilst preventing French reinforcements with the English navy, which is larger than the French one at the start of the game.

Another strategy is to remove France from the war by ceding land to them. If the player wishes to play a more isolationist England which does not really interfere with European affairs, it may be recommended to cede the lands in France which the French hold a core, both to remove them as a threat from England but also so that they will most likely not return until the French wish to control Calais (The only English province that France does not hold a core on at game start).

The final strategy is to build up the army and navy as outlined in the section for forming the Angevin Kingdom, as whilst the goal of the player may not be to deal with France directly, it serves as a strategy to remove them from the conflict somewhat effectively.

When subjugating Scotland, it may be advisable to also remove the Scottish core from theprovince of Mann, which can aid with increasing the opinion of Scotland and preventing events which can cause friction between Scotland and England relating to returning the core province. The player will not be able to annex The Isles if they choose to subjugate Scotland. Furthermore, reducingliberty desire of the Scottish can be achieved by destroying their army and navy before concluding the war, as well as repaying any debts that they might have through the subject interactions menu.

Following the subjugation of Scotland, The Isles may then be conquered (They may have already been integrated by the Scottish, which may be relatively simple as they are a weak nation who are unlikely to gain any strong allies. Scotland also holds cores on their provinces, and so ceding those provinces to Scotland can aid in both reducing their liberty desire and reducing the burden on the player’sadministrative power, which can be precious in the early game due to England starting with a 0,0,0ruler. Once this has been completed, the English may wish to further conquer the Atlantic islands if they wish to pursue colonisation of the New World.

##### Passing the Act of Union of Scotland[edit|edit source]

The Act of UnionParliamentary Debate holds similar requirements to the debate regarding France to form the Angevin Kingdom, requiringAdministrative Technology Level 10, however this Act formsGreat Britain, depending on the outcome of theDestiny of England mission. Scotland must hold less than 15 provinces however, when compared to France who must have less than 40. Once this issue has been passed, the nation of Great Britain will be formed.

#### Forming the Angevin Kingdom[edit|edit source]

Forming theAngevin Kingdomcan be difficult for players who are less experienced with the game, however, once the nation is formed it has the potential to become extremely powerful, dominating Western Europe with claims on the regions of France, Iberia, Italy and the Southern half of the Low Countries (As of 1.37.5). Once the Angevin Kingdom has formed, the player is given the option to releaseSpain,ItalyandIreland(provided they do not already exist) aspersonal union states through theParliament debatesdebates for the three nations separately. This is alongside the subjugation ofScotland. The Angevin Kingdom requires that the player reachesAdministrative Technologylevel 10 before forming the nation, generally meaning that the player will reach the mid 1500s before forming the nation unless he switch out of the English Parliament reform thus bypassing any technological requirements and French province limit.

##### Idea Groups[edit|edit source]

Potential idea groups that may be suggested for the Angevin Kingdom's formation may be those of influence, which will aid in reducing the liberty desire of the French crown as well as increasing diplomatic reputation as is required by the Act of Union. Other suggestions for the idea groups when forming the Angevin Kingdom are those of Offensive and Quality in order to increase army quality and to further aid with consolidating France and further progression as the Angevin Kingdom, providingdiscipline,siege ability andleader bonuses. Once the Angevin Kingdom has been formed, it may be advisable to take Administrative ideas due to the high cost of controlling the land within France, which can number up to500 development by the time of integration as well as the lands within the British Isles.

##### Securing the French Union[edit|edit source]

The early game presents two options when the Surrender of Maine event is triggered. The first option is to fightFranceand their allies for the subjugation of France using the casus belli from the Hundred Years War, and the second option is to return Maine to the French in order to prepare for a later French war to subjugate them (The casus belli is obtained from the English eventDestiny of England). The first option may not be suited for newer players due to the need to balance the Hundred Years War and theWars of the Roses Disaster, which will most likely fire at the same time. The second option can allow for the player to prepare for the war more in comparison to fighting the war against the French within the first year of the game start (Mean time to happen for theSurrender of Maineevent is 12 months).

Starting at the 11th of November 1444, the player may be advised to commence with an army and navy buildup to prepare for the inevitable French confrontation, for which it may be recommended to build enough transports to move entire armies from England to the European continent for the later war. Furthermore, it is recommended for the player to find strongallies who have also declared the French their rival (This may beAustria,Hungary,Castile,PortugalorBurgundy). These allies will not be useful in the case of the Hundred Years War CB, as the English are the aggressor and so allies cannot be called into the war. The Hundred Years War casus belli was updated in 1.36, where England must control the capital of France (normally Paris), and France must control the province of Maine. However, the French allies are automatically called into the war due to it being a defensive war. These can include  Scotland,Switzerland,Savoy,Provence, orAragon(although there may be numerous other nations that the French may ally with).

In the case that the player wishes to subjugate France in the Hundred Years War, then it is recommended that the player first attack Scotland (if they are allied to the French) due to the threat that they present to the English homeland, which can easily ruin a war if left unchecked. They may then be removed from the war with either land, ducats, war reparations, or any other terms which may help with the present war. Following this focus, the French may have occupied the entirety of English France, or almost the enterity depending on the circumstances. As a result, it may be advised that the player request fleet basing rights and military access from a neighbouring nation to France, which could be a number of nations, as well as acquiring mercenaries which match or fall underneath the number of transports which the player owns or is building. The French army alongside their vassals regularly numbers between35,000 to 45,000 soldiers, meaning that these mercenaries may be necessary in order to finish the war. In this case, it may be advised to approach from either Castile or Burgundy, given that they regularly are also rivals of the French and so may offer these accesses freely. In the case that France has occupied the entirety of English France, the player may face their entire army being defeated if they are to suffer a defeat, meaning this strategy can be somewhat precarious.

Following the landing, liberating the lands of England may be advisable in order to regain the tax and production income from the provinces, which can aid with preventing continuous loans. Following this, the removal of the French allies may further reduce the willingness of the French and their vassals to continue the war. The fort in Chartres may cause some issues with securing the war goal, as well as other forts such as Auvergne.

The second strategy takes more time, which may lead to the French strengthening their position on the continent and so potentially a more difficult war if it was to be fought by solely the player, however the extra time that is offered by the game will allow the player to address theWars of the Roses disaster before the war to force the union on the French. This can be advantageous as a change of ruler can result in the personal union breaking before the player has been able to secure positive relations with the French. This strategy relies upon the allies that the player can gather and who they can call into the war against the French. Generally, the Iberians (Castile, Aragon and Portugal) can be called into the war with favours, as well as potentially the Austrians. These allies can essentially be used as shields who can help in their manpower pools and troop numbers against the French whilst Scotland is removed from the war as stated in the above section. Generally, advancing from the south with allies such as Castile or Portugal can avoid the player being trapped by the French and being destroyed by them, allowing for them to sweep north against the French and secure the personal union against the French.

When securing the French lands, theunion must first be enforced (which uses60% warscore), after which it may be advisable to retake the cores of Armagnac and Maine in order to weaken the French further, which reduces their force limit and taxation ability and to benefit your own. Furthermore, it may be advisable to demand for money or war reparations in order to further reduce the income of France, which will help to secure the throne. This will also complete the mission Seize France's Throne. This mission grantsprestige, as well as+20% manpower recovery speed and-10% liberty desire, alongside permanent claims on the remainder of the France region.

Following the union, it is critical that the player begins to improve relations with their new French subject in order to secure the union. The liberty desire of their new subject will not be a worry for a time due to the truce now in place with the French. As a result, they will be unable to have other nations support independance. In order to prevent the nation breaking away on the death of the Monarch however, the player must improve relations until they reach0 or above. As the relations begin to improve, it may be advisable to reduce the French liberty desire by supporting loyalists in France, further reducing theirliberty desire by 20%.

##### Dealing with the Dukes of France[edit|edit source]

There are four missions which must be completed in order for the Angevin Kingdom to be formed following the subjugation of France, which are those of Secure the Duke's Crowns, Shatter French Nobility, Seize Burgundy, and finally The Angevin Kingdom. The dukes of France are those of Burgundy, Brittany and Provence. The mission Seize France's Throne grants claims on the provinces of Burgundy, Brittany and Provence within the region of France. Generally, Burgundy should be dealt with last due to their strength as a result of their own subjects making them tricky to deal with alongside a rebellious France. A consideration for the player to take into account is that theAct of Union Parliament Issuerequires that France holds less than 40provinces, therefore expanding France in order to reduce their liberty desire can be achieved to an extent.

Of the other two missions that can be completed, Shatter French Nobility can be prepared for through the triggering of the Parliament issue when it is available, which causes the loss of one stability and the spawning of Pretender Rebels in the capital area of France. This issue also causes-20% nobility influence,+60 Yearly Tax Income and+15% Manpower Recovery Speed in order to help with dealing with the rebels. The player can deal with these somewhat quickly following the war, and this mission can be swiftly completed once France'sliberty desire is reduced below 30%

The second of the two missions is that of Secure the Duke's Crowns. This mission requires that Provence and Brittany either are a subject of England or that they do not hold any land within the France region. The conquest of Brittany can be somewhat complex depending on the diplomatic situation of the country, who may ally nations rivalled to France as a result of them being threatened by them. This could lead to either a swift conquest of the country, or some time to restore the treasury of england and regain some manpower before conquering the country. The other nation of Provence possesses the personal union subject ofLorraine, which can cause complications when attempting to secure the France region. Whilst they rarely manage to acquire many strong allies, they may still present a minor challenge, especially with a rebellious France. The nation of Lorraine causes complications due to the Act of Union requiring the conquest of the entire France region, and if they are left free of Provence, can cause complications due to them being a member of theHoly Roman Empire. Therefore, it can be recommended to annex Lorraine in the initial war with Provence in order to prevent this complication. This will take more than one war to fully annex Provence, however the first war should leave them sufficiently in debt to allow for an easy second conquest. This mission will aid in the subjugation of France through it granting+50 opinion and+15 trust.

The final mission is that of Seize Burgundy. Burgundy is a stronger nation than that of the other French Dukes, possessing four subjects inNevers,Brabant,HollandandFlanders, alongside the ability to gather a number of allies due to their relative strength and development. As a result, this may lead to multiple wars and multiple conquests of the areas needed. There is a possibility of theBurgundian Inheritanceoccuring, provided that the English can remove the possibility of Burgundy declaring them as a rival and being able to secure aroyal marriage. However, this is still left to random chance as to whether or not it could be secured in this way. Furthermore, it is not necessary to annex any subjects (besides Nevers) of burgundy, meaning that all warscore can be focused on their lands within the French region, being those of Picardie, West Burgundy and East Burgundy, which can be taken in any order. Upon completing this mission, all three of these areas will lose 10 years ofseparatism, as well ascore creation cost being reduced by 25% and a further loss of 10 years ofSeparatism.

##### Passing the Act of Union Parliamentary Debate[edit|edit source]

Following the completion of these missions, the player must own the France region either themselves or by a subject, the French must have at least150 opinion of the player, and the player must also have+2 diplomatic reputation. The conquest of France from this point consists of simply conquering the province of Avignon fromThe Papal State. This conquest can either be simple or complex depending on the alliance situation of both the player and the Papal State, however, the province must be acquired in some way before the Act of Union can be passed. The missionThe Angevin Kingdomwill result in the province of Anjou gaining 3 of eachdevelopment type, as well as local modifiers. The mission then unlocks the Act of Union, as well as granting claims on the North of Italy, the Aragonese lands in Iberia (besides the Balearic Islands) as well as the South of the Low Countries and Alsace. The Act of Union requires that England holds a number of provinces on both the English homeland (not including Scotland or Ireland) alongside some French provinces. Once the Act is passed through the Parliament system, the Angevin Kingdom will be formed.

#### Colonial Britain[edit|edit source]

Colonial England generally is a more defensive choice as it doesn't require direct conflict with continental Europe until later into the game. In this variation securing a strong economic base is vital to rapid colonization. One method is to conquer Scotland using the previously mentioned strategy of declaring war on one of the Irish minors allied with Scotland and including Scotland into the war as a co-belligerent. This will prevent a struggle with France.

##### Increasing early colonial range[edit|edit source]

The challenge with an England centered colonization campaign is that they struggle to reach colonial regions until later technologies. A helpful way to increase England'scolonial range is to look toward neighboringNorwayand its Icelandic provinces and hiring a colonial advisor. Norway has a few neighboring provinces to the British isles. England can get involved in a war with their suzerain,Denmark, by supporting the independence ofSweden. An advantage to pursuing colonization through a northern strategy is that it confines England to the Northern portion of the Americas, away from the likely Iberian colonies in South America.

A second way to increase colonial range is through conquest of parts of the Iberian Peninsula. Early fabrication of claims and rivaling of Castile can enable the release of potential vassals likeLeón(+1colonist as level 4 national idea) and securing the provinces of Galicia and The Canaries to put England closer to the colonizable regions of Africa and South America. Notably this method puts England in direct conflict for colonies with allyPortugaland now rivalCastile.

Recommended idea groups for this direction areExplorationandExpansion.
