TheMughal Empirewas a powerful Indianised Turco-MongolMuslimstate, founded in the early 16th century by Babur, aTimuridprince and descendant of Tamerlane. At its height in the early 18th century, the empire controlled almost all of the Indian subcontinent and quarter of the world's economy before internal struggles allowed regional powers to take hold (such as the Maratha and Sikh Empires) and the Empire fractured. Its final stronghold, Delhi, was annexed by the British in 1858 after the Indian Rebellion. In-game, the Mughals may be selected in a later historical start or formed by any Iranian or Central Asian state which controls certain provinces in India.

### Diwan[edit|edit source]

The Mughals have access to theDiwanmechanic, which replaces the regularculturepromotion mechanics. Instead of spending diplomatic monarch power to promote cultures into accepted status, the Mughals can 'assimilate' cultures. To assimilate any particular culture the Mughals must 'directly own' (hence no need for a core, but own themselves and not via subjects) every single province of that culture (e.g. Afghan or Khorasani; including uncolonized provinces, but excluding provinces in colonial regions). The moment every province comprising that culture is owned a popup message will confirm its successful 'permanent' assimilation into the empire. Therefore, subsequently to receiving such assimilation confirmation the province could for example be granted to a subject for them to core.

Assimilation of a specific culture is permanent; if any owned province is subsequently lost, released as part of a vassal or given away, its culture will remain assimilated - hence it will show as anaccepted culturein the province window. There is no cap on how many cultures may be assimilated, and assimilating an entire culture group grants a unique bonus, listed in the table below. Be aware that whereas the assimilation is permanent the culture 'group' bonuses are not. Bonuses only apply if the Mughals directly own every provinces in that group, the moment even just one province is owned by a vassal or anyone else the bonus will be lost until such province is owned again.

This disables being able to change primary culture, and the accept culture buttons. But one can still change the culture of provinces via the normal methods.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

Mughals are perhaps easiest to form with theTimurids. If you manage your vassals well enough, you can just invade the Kashmir Region and continue into Dehli from there to get the necessary provinces.

Your reward for forming Mughals are some very unique mechanics. The Mughal Divan bonuses, as explained above, push you to conquer entire culture groups, although getting any culture group early might prove difficult: India usually has a strong net of alliances between formidable regional powers which needs to be carefully managed, while the Ottomans will sooner or later try to get some Iranian culture provinces in Mesopotamia. Tartar and Atltaic to the north are spread out and usually some nation falls under the protection of Ming or Ottomans. If getting an entire culture group isn't feasible, try to collect just a culture: You still save the 100 dip you would have to spend to promote it as any other nation, and the provinces are immediately more productive as a result.

The other (semi)- unique mechanic is the Mansabdari System. The Nobility privilege "Mansabdar - Cavalry force" provides an impressive−50%cavalry cost, scaling withloyalty. With this much of a reduction in cost, it could be worth maxing out on the 75-85% cavalry ratio you'll have, if you can keep your nobility loyal. "Mansabdar - Military Service" giving up to−1%army tradition decay, scaling withinfluence, may not redefine your army the same way, but is still be worth picking, especially when the absolutism reduction does not apply yet. To increase Nobility loyalty/influence, Court Ideas and the Reform "General Estates" can aid. They will also help keep your other estates loyal, for example the Rajputs, who can serve as the supporting Infantry. If/once Absolutism is more important to you than cavalry, the "Mansabdar - Royal Rule" privilege increases maximum Absolutism by+10, at the cost of loyalty and army maintenance. It also reduces Nobility Influence, which can be used to remove other privileges quickly.

Another great interaction with Mansabdari System is theWar Economygovernment reform, as it reduces the cost of your cavalry (and other) regiments even further, while also making reducing war taxes  to-100%.

Mughal Ideas have among the best core creation cost reduction−25%, with another−10%available by assimilatingHindustani Culture. Administrative Ideas−25%can stack on top of those modifiers, reducing the administrative power used and time spend overextended. Any modifier here will make conquering the required provinces for Diwan cheaper and easier.

Mughal Missions mainly reward conquering specific regions by giving a 25 year buff and permanent claims on the next regions in the mission tree. Often times, a player has to make the choice between conquering cultures for the Diwan bonuses or conquering Regions for the mission tree. The rightmost mission branch is particularly capable of getting in the way of Diwan bonuses: The finishing mission "Viceroyalty of the Deccan?" makes Mughals releaseDeccanas a Vassal with−100%Liberty desire, and gives+5%Administrative Efficiency. It even gives Deccani Cores to any province, even ones Mughals did not own at the time, enabling "Reconquest" CBs. However, the area ceded to Deccan has provinces of 3 different culture groups by default, removing/preventing+5%Trade efficiency from Western Aryan,+5%production efficiency from eastern Aryan and+5%goods produced from Dravidian as Diwan bonuses until Deccan is annexed. Unlike with manually released Vassals, the full cores of Mughal are kept, making it possible to annex Deccan after 10 years at no / minimal cost.

The event also gives the option to relocated your capital instead, but this option is without the+5%Administrative Efficiency reward.
