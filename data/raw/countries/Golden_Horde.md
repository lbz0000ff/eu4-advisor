TheGolden Hordewas the western branch of the greatMongol Empire, and the dominant power in Eastern Europe and the western Eurasian steppe from the 13th to the early 15th centuries. By 1444, the Golden Horde has splintered into several squabbling successor states, which proved unable to resist the expansion of a newly unitedRussia. In-game, if any of these states manages to conquer the others, reunite the Tatars and subjugate Russia, they may reform the Golden Horde and reclaim the Mongol legacy. TheGreat Hordeis the best candidate for achieving this, as it is the most powerful of the successor states and is also the most legitimate, as the primary successor state to the Golden Horde.

### StrategyEdit

With the Golden Horde reformed, there are three different paths forward that one may decide to pursue and dedicate the majority of the campaign to. The inspiration for each can be found in the Golden Horde's name and itsSunni religion. The Golden Horde is effectively a Tatar version ofRussia, albeit one with less overall manpower and individually more capable cavalry troops.

#### For the Horde! (Eastward bound)Edit

The first possible path is the conventional one. By leaning into the "Horde" part of the Golden Horde and following its mission tree, one will embark on a great campaign eastwards, to reunite the rest of the Mongol Empire and eventually reform it. This path will see one fight mostly other hordes, with the endgoal of ultimately reconqueringMingChina to restore theMongol Empire. From there, the only remaining step is to successfully achieve what the Mongols could not accomplish the previous time; conquer all ofIndiaandJapanto establish uncontested hegemony over Asia.

To help facilitate this military campaign, useful idea groups to select includeOffensive andQuality, to make your Generals and troops more capable at fighting whatever you might face, whileAdministrative andHumanist will help with maintaining the realm and reducing unrest caused by wrong religion provinces. Because the Golden Horde's national ideas already give+2Tolerance of Heathens, adoptingHumanist ideas will give an additional+2Tolerance of Heathens and thereby allow them to reach the maximum possible tolerance value and thus effectively sideline the need to convert heathen provinces, as they will no longer have any penalties to unrest, taxation or production.

#### For the Gold! (Westward bound)Edit

The second possible path is more unconventional. By leaning into the "Golden" part of the Golden Horde and setting aside its mission tree, the Golden Horde can instead decide to head toward the west, with the equally ambitious goal of conquering all ofEuropeand looting its vast riches for themselves, achieving what Ögedei Khan was unable to before he suddenly died in 1241. If theGold Rushachievement was already completed, then why not continue with that theme by pillaging the european states and their inhabitants of everything they have of value?

Before one can even entertain the notion of expanding into europe, one must be aware of one potential hazard; Europe's provinces are fairly highly developed, and thus annexing territory in the region will very quickly build upAggressive Expansion, which risks triggering the formation of coalitions and making nations more hostile than they already are. This becomes especially severe when you conquer provinces in the Holy Roman Empire, as all aggressive expansion will be increased by+50%. Because you follow theSunni religion, theCuria controllermight even call a crusade against you! To counteract this, by combining theHorde andEspionage ideas, one can achieve a-30%reduction of AE impact, which, when combined withDiplomatic ideas to help with improving relations, will help to stave off potential coalitions and retaliatory actions. Additionally, Humanist ideas, which are again vital to avoid unrest due to Europe being of a heathenous religion, also tie well into Diplomatic ideas by giving you a combined+45%bonus to improving relations with other countries. From there, it will be a matter of trying of divide and conquer; try to isolate your targets and attack them while they are weak or vulnerable, expand gradually and wait to let Aggressive Expansion decay over time and use diplomacy to keep countries from attacking as much as possible. If needed, you can utilize the method of conquering provinces from a country, releasing them as avassal and then using the"Reconquest" CB to seize the vassal's remaining core provinces from other countries, as this generates less AE than outright conquest. If you defeat a country that is simultanously at war with another one, you could vassalize them and thus "inherit" their war and defeat their other opponent as well, taking down two targets in one fell swoop without the need for a separate war. If you manage to conquer all of Europe, then theoretically, by reforming out of being a Steppe Horde and converting to Christianity, you could reform theRoman Empirefor one of the most bizarre sequence of events possible. The Golden Horde conquered Russia, the self-proclaimed Third Rome, only to then later reform the original Rome themselves. How is that for historical irony? Alternatively, doing that could be interpreted as a western mirror image of the historical formation of theYuandynasty.

#### For the Faith! (Southward bound)Edit

The third possible path is the most unusual of the three. By leaning into the Golden Horde'sSunni religion and the Devout branch of the Religious Affairs section in the mission tree. The goal of this path is to work toward formingArabiaandUnifying Islamto supercharge yourMissionaries. This will require invading south intoCircassiaandGeorgia, then fighting your way through theAq QoyunluandQara QoyunluTribal Federations and finally striking down theMamluks(and potentially theOttomansandPersiaas well if they pose an obstacle) to conquer the Arabian peninsula and the Fertile Crescent. The Golden Horde can try to ally with the Mamluks, Ottomans or Persia as the situation permits to make expansion easier for as long as possible, but seeing as they desire many of the provinces the Tatars are aiming to take, those alliances will not be sustainable and eventually other allies will be needed to combat your erstwhile ones.

By culture-shifting to Egyptian culture, the Golden Horde may form Arabia and, if theFor the Horde!mission was completed (as a Sunni Horde), one will only need 10 out of the needed provinces to Unify Islam, which is within fairly easy reach if one conquers all of the territory needed to form Arabia as well as the provinces of Dagestan in the Caucasus, Samarkand in Khorasan, Constantinople in the Balkans and Thatta in India. The reward for doing all of this is as follows; by formingArabia,Unifying Islamand adoptingReligious ideas, the resulting Caliphate will have no less than+4Additional missionaries and+10%Missionary strength, in addition to a permanentCB and-20%Lower war score costs against other religions. The Caliphal Horde will be able to launch Jihads to conquer vast swathes of territory and then convert them at what could be described as an "industrial efficiency". This will award theDar al-Islamachievement and also create a good starting position for achieving theOne Faithachievement as well.
