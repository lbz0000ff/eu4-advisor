Ambition:

In Europe, the Ottomans have defeated the Crusaders in the Battle of Varna, killing Wladyslaw, thePolishandHungarianking.Byzantiumnow lies exposed and Hungary has been forced on the defensive, but with the fortress of Belgrade, still serves as a strong bulwark against further Ottoman expansion. The lesser princes in between can survive only by playing the great powers off one another. Further south, Skanderbeg leadsAlbaniain revolt against the Ottomans, supported byVenice, which still dominates the seas.

The realm ruled by the Ottoman dynasty is not yet a centralized state in 1444. The sultan's authority is enforced only by his small household of servants, recruited from slaves captured in war and from the 'devshirme', the periodic conscription of Christian youths from the Balkan countryside. These are the famous Janissaries, who will in time grow into a powerful standing army. The Ottomans have come to power with the military backing of numerous marcher-lords, still highly autonomous on the Balkan frontier, while the administration lies in the hands of the Turkish aristocracy, literate in the ways of statecraft, but largely independent of the sultan's authority.

All this is about to change. Having defeated the Crusader armies, the old sultan Murad II is now on his way back to his estates in Manisa, leaving the government in the hands of the young Mehmed II. It is he who will go on to transform the Ottoman state into an empire, acquiring Constantinople as an imperial capital and establishing a centralized administration to rein in the marcher-lords and old Turkish families. For 'The Grand Turk', there can be no compromise – the House of Osman is to be the supreme authority in the land. The only question is this: where will his armies turn to first?

Europa Universalis IV starts on November 11th 1444, the day after theOttomansvictory over the Christian alliance atVarna. They begin owning most ofAnatoliaand the southernBalkans. For new players, the early game will likely be focused on consolidating the country's position in those regions. Internally, the country is divided between theOrthodox andSunni faiths. Economically, they hold the majority of the Constantinople node trade power with very easy access to the rest.

In theBalkans, the important trade center of Constantinople and the region of Morea are held by the declining nation ofByzantiumand its vassal,Athens. The Aegean Sea is controlled mainly byVeniceandGenoahindering control of the Constantinopletrade node. TheAnatolianregion is a home to several Turkish Beyliks. Further east, in the Taurus and Caucasus mountains, the tribes ofAq QoyunluandQara Qoyunlucontrol some outlying Ottoman territory, and shield the Ottomans from theirhistorical rival, theTimurids. To the south, historical and trade rival theMamlukscontrol a sprawling trade empire that extends into Africa, while their vassalHejazcontrols the holy cities of Medina and Mecca. To the west, the Balkan nations are ripe for the taking -Moldaviais the only safe nation due to it being guaranteed byPolandwhich blocks expansion. Further expansion into the west is halted byAustriaandHungary, while across the Adriatic,Italyis mostly protected by theHoly Roman EmperorandAragon.

The Ottomans have a uniquegovernmenttype named the Ottoman Government. This government is in many ways synonymous with the royal household, but with a harem feature that ensures that there will never be a lack of heirs for the Ottoman ruler. The Ottomans follow theHanafi school ofIslam.

### Ottoman Government[edit|edit source]

The Ottoman Government is a unique government type with fixed dynasty and the following benefits:

- +0.5Yearly legitimacy
- −10%Unjustified demands
- +150Governing capacity
- +5Maximum absolutism
This government is permanently lost if the country is no longer Muslim or if the government type is changed during theRevolutiondisaster or by revolutionary rebels enforcing their demands.

The harem helps ensure that there will never be a lack of heirs for the empire.  An event triggers if the current ruler has reached 30 years of age and has no heir which allows the player choose an heir for the throne from a random list of three choices, being able to view their monarch stats prior to the choice.

Normal vassals can be turned into eyalets via the "Promote to Eyalet" diplomatic action. Additionally, the "Ottoman Campaign of Conquest"casus bellienables the "Establish Eyalet" peace treaty, which will turn the target nation into a eyalet subject. ### Missions[edit|edit source]

Practically all of the Ottomans missions revolve around expanding their borders. In the early stages of the game, many of the missions revolve around reclaiming the lands of Anatolia, as well as the iconic mission to conquer the City of the World's Desire: Constantinople or Conquer the Crusader's Last Bastion (Rhodes). Further missions will request the Ottoman Empire to expand and conquer the regions of Balkans, North Africa, Syria, Egypt, Iraq, as well as individual provinces - all to increase trading power in the spice trade and to unitelands against Christendom.

### Events[edit|edit source]

Many historical events are the aftermath from the expansion and government reforms of the Ottoman Empire - such events can revolve around the relationship between the Ottoman Empire and the trade states ofVeniceandGenoa, the left-over GreekOrthodox Patriarchate, enacting reforms that might not be popular with everyone, or suppressing nationalistic rebels.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

#### Overview[edit|edit source]

The Ottomans begin the game in 1444 in theAnatolian militarytechnology groupat level 3 for Administration, Diplomacy and Military withFeudalismembraced. If you rush to Military level 5,Ottomansshould have the strongest infantry units, comparing toWestern,Muslim andEastern units.

Because they have very strong missions, easy avenues of expansion and some very good bonuses, events and decisions, they usually become the strongest and the most developed nation in the world very fast. At this stage the question becomes how big can the Ottomans grow. Other thanAustria, Rivals may includeCommonwealth,Russia,Great Britain,France,MingandSpain.

- 60% Ratio. As a Sunni nation, Cavalry numbers may be up to 60% of a total army size, excludingartillery, before theInsufficient Support penaltyis incurred. Note that this will be reduced to the standard 50% Cavalry ratio upon conversion to another religion.
- Anatolian units: seeAnatolian land units. Early gameAnatolian units (up to military tech 15) are superior to Western by 1 pip. Notably, Janissaries (infantry at military tech 9) have 1 offensive fire pip; the only other tech groups to get fire pips this early areIndian andChinese (who have a much harder time embracing institutions). Conversely, Anatolian cavalry has no fire pips until tech 18, compared to 10 for Western. However, at higher techs cavalry has very little use, so it might be best to simply cut the cav out of your armies in favor of infantry, Janissary infantry, and of course more cannons.
#### Before Starting the Timer[edit|edit source]

When starting a new game with the Ottomans, you should hire ten Janissary regiments (costing 3military manaeach), get ageneral, give out al least twoprivilegesto the new Janisariesestate("Sultan Appointed Aghas" and "Ensure Disciplined Training" are recommended) and build as many galleys as you can to kicksart your mission tree and unlock three missions from the get-go and open two more immediately. Only three galleys are enough to unlock the Ottoman Navy mission, but it is recommended to build more, since you will need them against naval superpowers likeVenice,AragonandCastile.

You will start with an available cheaper level two Natural Scientist (administrative advisor), Ali al-Qushji, so hire him. You are free to hire adiplomatic advisor too, preferably a Diplomat or a Statesman, butdo nothire amilitary advisor at the start, since an early event will make the cheaper level two Commandant Urban available. Hire him as soon as the event happens, and you will get four cannons for free from the unlocked mission. Keep in mind that Ottomans andFranceare the only two nations that can get cannons beforeMilitary technology 7.

Not changing religion and staying Sunni is strongly recommended. Ottomans start with Dhimmi Autonomy privilege already granted at the start, so capturing non-Muslim land will not create religious disunity problems. Changing the religion will also lose you so many advantages of the Ottoman government, such as Janissaries, Dhimmi estate, pashas, eyalets, harem system and extra governing capacity.

#### Alliances[edit|edit source]

Although Ottomans are strong enough that they do not really need anyallies, they also have lots of great choices in that regard. You should allyCrimeafrom the get-go, because if you complete the "Crimean Destiny" mission before "the Fate of the Crimean Khanate" event happens, they will become yourmarch as a result of that event, instead of a tributary. Also, your guarantee onRagusais unnecessary; revoke it to open another relation slot. Because you are a Muslim nation,royal marriagesare perfectly safe (you will not fall under apersonal union) and you may use them to secure alliances with any Muslim nation.

Bohemiais a good possible ally, who will join your wars againstAustria,HungaryandVenice. If they are not rivaled to you, you can improve relations withPolandto ally them, although they usually pickBohemiaas a rival, so you may need to choose only one of them if that happens.Francewill often ally you and help your wars with strong nations likeCastile,AragonandAustria, but be careful not to ally them before they finish their war with England, becuase you want to make Constantinople your capital as soon as possible and you need to be at peace to take that decision. But it is recommended that you allyFranceas soon as possible after their war and to not let them become your rival. You may also allyMuscovyas soon as you discover them, although do not expect a loyal ally. You will later clash with them and they will become a formidable enemy after they formRussia.

But none of these alliances are going to last, because you will start to desire their lands. Instead, it is much better to use your diplomatic relation slots for the nations you can diplomaticallyvassalize and promote toeyalets. Eyalets are better than vassals because they do not occupy diplomatic relation slots (unless there is aroyal marriage) and they give bonus manpower and money. But keep in mind that promoting a vassal significantly stalls the annexation. So do not promote anyvassals which might block your expansion avenues toeyalets (to annex eyalets, you need to turn them intocore eyalets first and wait for another ten years after that), but annex such vassals as soon as the option becomes available.

#### Early Moves[edit|edit source]

When you start the game, wait for the "Crusade of Varna" event (which will also happen very quickly), which gives you permanent claims onByzantiumin addition to some bonuses through a mission. AttackByzantiumimmmediately and full annex both Byzantium and Athens and white peace any allies in war. They will not get strong allies and you want to capture these allies' lands too, later. Do not core Constantinople/Kostantiniyye, as a very strong decision called "Make Kostantiniyye Your Capital", which will core it for free, turns it into a Sunni Turkish city and promotes you into an empire becomes available as soon as you end your war. #### Goals After Early Moves[edit|edit source]

From here, the possibilities for theOttomansare endless. Some players may try to Unify Islam, which involves conquering holy sites (provinces) and converting all of the player's provinces to Muslim. Some players may seek the "Definitely the Sultan of Rum" or  "Parisian Pasha" achievements. They are not that difficult, even for a new player. All the player will need to do is to manage aggressive expansion well, keep up on tech and institutions, and keep expanding. The Ottoman mission tree provides a huge amount of permanent claims and many places are worth taking later on.

After getting at least 6 provinces fromMamluks, you gain an invasion casus belli on them. No matter the case, always attack the Mamluks as soon as your truce expires. When you use the invasion CB, you need to siege down Cairo/Al-Qahirah and keep it sieged for two years. If you can get %99 warscore, they will automatically turn into an eyalet. Do not sign a peace them before the event which makesMamluksyoureyalet happens. Your missions will give you another invasion CB onTunis, which is recommended to be used immediately if there is no truce. In that war, if they joined your war, vassalizeGranadafor a mission and white peaceMorocco, against whom you will get a new invasion CB if you have successfully subjugatedTunis. FeedFezzan's provinces to Tunis and build a marketplace in Tripoli in order to open a Tunisian mission which gives them (and in proxy, to their overlord, you) a free province in Sahara, which will help with your later expansion into Sahel region.

All the nations in the Arabian peninsula will accept diplomatical vassalization except most probablyHormuzandYemen, but you want a directly owned coast to the Indian Ocean for further expansion opportunities. You may want to attack a few coastal ones likeHaasaandHormuzto allow you further expansion, but try to diplo-vassalize poorer nations in the desert and promote them to eyalets as much as possible. Keep rivalries in mind when doing so and try to avoid royal marriages to not unnecessarily clog your diplo relation slots. If you or your subjects own enough land in the Arabian Peninsula, you will gain an invasion CB onYemen.

Genoaowns some of the Greek islands and a few provinces near what is today's southern Ukraine. You want their lands in these regions, but it is advised to fight them directly only after the Shadow Kingdom event happens, otherwise the Emperor of theHRE(usuallyAustria) will protect Genoa. Because the Black Sea provinces (Caffa/Kefe, Matrega/Hunkala and Azov, to be exact) will also become yours in the same Crimean Succesion event, if Genoa ever joins a war of yours, feel free to feed these regions toCrimea.

A single successful war withHungaryending by transferring their Balkan lands and possibly also transferringCroatiafor yourself will unlock a mission which lets you make Hungary your eyalet. However, because Hungary usually tends to allyAustria, a strong European ally will probably be required for this war and any later wars which could invovle the Emperor.France,Poland,Bohemiaor evenMuscovycan decide to ally the Ottomans and help in the wars against the common enemyAustria. If Austria declares a Restoration of Union war, take that mission before Austria finishes that war, but this depends on RNG. IfAustriawins after you take the mission, can makeAustriayour eyalet, instead ofHungary. Be careful not to take anything else in that war to keep the truce short and to be able to still make Hungary an eyalet. If you lose the invasion CB, you can use the old strategy against Hungary and releaseTransylvania,NitraandCroatiaas vassals from them for possible reconquests. If that does not happen, you make Hungary your eyalet and get claims on Austria's lands, which can be used to dismantle the HRE.

It is possible to get Italian states after theThe Shadow Kingdom eventsby either diplo-vassalizing small Italian states and conqueringNaplesifAragonloses it. Owning land in Italy could get some easier institution process, as the colonization and printing press might be slow if the player conquer too much Asian or African lands. On the other hand, the lands in Europe are more highly developed and there are lots of nations of the same faith, so these lands create more AE and the player should not be as greedy when taking land there.

After getting these possessions, the player will be much more powerful than any nation in the world and you may even go on to conquer the world. You will have many expansion routes now. #### Converting to Orthodox[edit|edit source]

Ottomans can convert to Orthodox in several ways:

- The quickest way occurs prior to annexing any territory by sending a missionary to anOrthodox province triggers enough unrest to create a possible rebellion. Ottomans are sufficiently Orthodox to be able to convert by accepting the rebels demands. However, if this is done the Ottomans lose out on the decision tomake Constantinople the capitalmissing 11 development points. The upside is that Constantinople will not need to be converted.
- A second way to convert and securing the extra 11 development from themake Constantinople the capitaldecision is by conqueringByzantiumand at least two of the four nations ofAthensandAlbania,Theodoro, orTrebizond. After Constantinople is secured and the decision clicked sending a missionary to anyOrthodox province with unrest below 0 will create a rebellion that will allow accepting demands to convert to Orthodox. Religious rebels generated in the province of Trebizond have the advantage of being able to force convert Anatolia while completing sieges on provinces. A rebel generated in Edirne or Kirkkilise are the closest to Constantinople and can force convert that province - which otherwise can not be converted until a combination ofpatriarch authority, an inquisitor andReligious ideasare taken.
- Both methods should be done prior to the annexation of the Anatolian Beyliks as the extra provinces will reduce the percentage of Orthodox.
Note, there are two handicaps to this as reducedreligious unity incurs acorruption penalty and initially Orthodox are unable to convertSunni provinces.

If the Dhimmiestatehas low loyalty, revoking the heathen provinces granted to them will spawn zealots of the province's religion. As soon as the game starts,the Impose New Religious Tax interaction from the Estate tab can be taken, reducing Dhimmi loyalty to 35. Many Orthodox provinces start out as belonging to the Dhimmi estate, and can be revoked after a year has passed. The Zealots spawned will convert every non-Orthodox province they occupy toOrthodox if the occupied province is not within a fort's Zone of Control, including Konstantiniyye after Make Constantinople the Capital decision has been taken, if the fort in Edirne is destroyed.. Zealots will not be able to cross the strait to Anatolia as there are almost always AI ships in the Sea of Marmara. They instead will march all the way around the Black Sea until they are defeated in battle by a nation along the way, or arrive back into Ottoman lands. The player can annexTrebizondand send a missionary to the province (as outlined above) after granting it to the Dhimmi estate, then revoke the estate ownership after a year if they wish to force convertSunni Anatolia prior to switching to Orthodox.

But staying Sunni is also an option, because you will lose the amazing Ottoman Government form if you do choose to convert.
