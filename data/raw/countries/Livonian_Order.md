### Livonian Path or Crusader Path[edit|edit source]

After completing the Mission "Strengthen Our Authority", the Mission tree has 2 branching paths: A Livonian Path focused on colonization, developing Livonia and the Baltic Trade, and a Crusader Path focused on expanding eastward, cleansing of Orthodox Heresy and crushing the Reformation. The Crusader Path Missions all require the country to beCatholic and encourage staying a theocracy, the Livonian path Missions can be done with any Religion and encourage becoming a Monarchy.

Every Event has 3 options to choose from, anAdministrative Option, aDiplomatic Option and aMilitary Option, each increasing their respective "Focus". Once the Mission the"The Livonian Monarchy" is completed, the"The Government of Livonia" event will let you choose a special Government reform, depending on the Focus. The individual choices in the events have their own costs and benefits, but the available government reforms are only determined by the total amount of Focus points.

For example, if you pick1 administrative(always the first) ,0 diplomatic(always 2nd) and5military(always 3rd) options in the 6 events, the Focus will have the values of1/0/5.

The exact values of Focus needed for every Reform can be found on theGovernment Reforms - MonarchyPage. The events in game will also display the values needed if you hover over any option, as well as what values your focus will have if you select that option.

Regardless of Focus, the Event "The Government of Livonia" will always give you the option to reform into the"Livonian Monarchy", and the option to keep your current government reform.

Some Notes :

- No option is strictly better then another, though some reduce yourGoverning Capacity. Try to match the available Government Reforms to your playstyle.
- The "Livonian Enlightened Monarchy" is the most specific of the Reforms, requiring 2 points to be put into every Category of your Focus. Going for this Reform means you wont get any other options besides it and the standard "Livonian Monarchy"
- You can get the most Options possible by putting 5 points of your Focus into Dip (the last point does not matter). This will unlock the "Livonian States General", "Livonian Admiralty", "Livonian States Council" and "Livonian Elective Monarchy" reforms, as well as the standard "Livonian Monarchy".
### Strategy[edit|edit source]

You start in a windy corner of Europe hemmed in on 3 sides by some powerful rivals. The Livonian Order is weak early on but if you can get going, your national ideas and unique government reforms are extremely powerful.

The early weakness is in part due to the Bishoprics and theirPrivileges, you must either spend money, pay devotion and fight a rebel army, or manually remove them.

Another part is the lack of easy expansion. The easiest way is probablyNovgorodto the east, especially if they are already fightingMuscovy, but this will send you straight into conflict with the latter.Rigais generally friendly, but a poor ally, and should be dealt with before they join the HRE via their Missions, though they can have some nasty alliances as well. TheTeutonic Orderis friendly, but will get you in conflict withPolandif you ally them - not that they and/orLithuaniawont attack you otherwise.

If an independent Lithuania remains peaceful then don’t provoke them and work on Novgorod. If Lithuania has a bad war going through jump in and take some land. Main goal is either to have a strong Prussian ally or to hold the Prussian lands yourself to advance your missions. Ally in the region, maybe supportSwedenindependence, depending on the specifics of your game. Keep gobbling up Russian Territory. If Lithuania attacks, defend and wait for your chosen local powerhouse to help you out. Aim for White Peaces, you actually want Lithuania as a buffer for later.

For ideas I actually recommend a double Economic start with Trade and Economic followed by Quality and Religious. Keep rolling East and South towards the Crimea. Convert along the way. Your ideas are great for that and you can use your influence to become papal controller.

Once you get going you are basically a catholic Russia. You can betray Sweden and take Provinces in Scandinavia, but converting the heretics in the east and the free Papal influence that comes with it should take priority.

Expectation wise it's very hard for a Livonian world conquest or mega nation.
