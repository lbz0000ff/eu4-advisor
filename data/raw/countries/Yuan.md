### Strategy[edit|edit source]

Re-establishing the Yuan dynasty brings great rewards due to its superb idea set, which combines military and expansionary power. There are, however, a number of disadvantages if one becomes the Emperor of China. Becoming the emperor changes technology type into Chinese. This is a rather severe malus if one is playing as a horde nation, especially with the Cossacks Expansion enabled. Forming Yuan will remove the permanent CB on all neighboring countries and the raze mechanic, the latter arguably the strongest single mechanic in the game, single handedly solving all Monarch Point concerns, allowing constant truce breaks, and even managing over extension. For greatest blobbing potential, the player should hold off on the decision to change government type, if they ever change at all.

One can avoid these disadvantages by instead dismantling the Mandate of Heaven by annexing the current Emperor without taking the Mandate. The requirement for forming Yuan will then be to have empire rank instead of being Emperor of China. This allows one to retain one's government type and tech group after forming Yuan. One of the easiest ways to go about this is playing asOirat, which has a mission that makes the initial war with Ming very easy. Losing the war will most likely send Ming into a doom spiral that will make China easy pickings later on. One can work on obtaining the required provinces between wars with Ming, or more likely the Chinese minors that will declare independence from Ming.

If player feels daring(this is a more easier method but hard at the beginning of the game), it is possible to play as Mongolia and gain independence by either allying Uzbek or other neighbouring nations. This way, player avoids any unintended rivalries and has the chance of vassalising or making the neigbouring countries tributaries which will allow them to directly forming Yuan through a more diplomaic way and keeping the economic crisis that maintaining a huge army for war at bay. Player can declare way on Ming through vassal state's CB or Mongolia's own CB before 1480 and take the necesarry regions for forming Yuan. This will diminish the power of Ming and provoke revolts. Player, should try to take advantage of it and declare war on Ming to take the Mandate of Heaven. Note: Avoid declaring war on Ming between your first war to conquer the Northern China and Mandate of Heaven war. You will be tempted to threaten with war option if you played cleverly, but do not go that way and postpone your mandate war, because Ming will struggle with rebellions and possibly lose to revolts. If Ming is divided into many Kingdoms, try to vassalise or make tributary at least one of them. Preferably one that pops up in Southern China, so you save yourself from any war to conquer important port cities.

There is a certain amount of poetry involved in Chinese history, and that is a not insignificant motivator, but if one's goal is a World Conquest, be very careful. The Imperialism CB comes only much later, and taking lands without severely tanking your Diplomatic Monarch Points will either require laborious work from your precious diplomats, or the Holy War CB from Religious Ideas.

Another way of expanding to west or approaching Americas is through vassalisation of Japan or England in very late game. If you used a diplomatic approach to expand, you will have countless CBs and be able to force tributary on almost everyone after a certain time period. For myself, I was able to establish tributary on Muscovy through diplomacy as I already had many vassals or tributaries under me in India, Southern China and in Persia. After having certain amount of tributaries or powerful vassals with lots of armies, everyone sees you as a powerful nation and wish to be protected by you. I was able to do it as early as in 1485. You just have to be patient and let your tributaries do the job for you. You can vassalise them after you pass 8 decisions in Empire and get the option to elevate the tributary status. If you adjust your mandates well, you will have enough mandate to vassalise most of your tributaries before they become too developed to go over the boundary of 100 mandate. I was even able to vassalise Genoa and Hungary from all the way from China. Just read the requirements and look for an opening. Most restricting thing you would have is with your ability to core far away regions and that can be altered through even little vassals on faraway lands. So, try to get a vassal in Europe, even Malta Knights would do. Since it will be your first encounter, they will have no Agressive Expension against you or they will neither dislike you, so you can easily ally and vassalise them. Most important thing you should do is always assign at least 3 of your diplomats to improve relations with outraged countries and you will be surprised at the end and always try to increase your diplomatic reputation. I was able to get 10 through advicor and ideas. If you do not play in a diplomatic way as I do, you will have to rely heavily on wars and it will be too expensive. Bear in mind that you have to go through India to conquer western countries and believe me, you won't even be able to annex more than 7 regions in a single war and it won't even get you a bit closer to Ottomans or Persia. You do not have to be passive, but you have to be diplomatic most of the time and try to convert the country to Confucius from Tengri belief. It is very easy to do that and you will have no problem with rebellion over it. Being in Tengri belief is not efficient and you can give eunuchs a right which would give you a boost in your relations with other countries if you become a part of Confucius belief. As to the matter of how you can manage your trade, you will suffer a lot until you have developed port cities in your hands. You will have to choose a trade idea to empower your developed regions' trade value and power. I gave a short method above, it should be efficient well enough.

Though, if the player is beginning as a non horde, there is something to be said about conquering Altaic land, culture flipping and forming Yuan for the claims on all of the China super-region, hopefully setting up for Absolutism with all the nice Chinese land and the trade node in Beijing.
