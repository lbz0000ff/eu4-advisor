### Strategy[edit|edit source]

#### Playing ways and expanding routes[edit|edit source]

Switzerland can stay passive and focus on their army and economy while expanding through events for getting Three Leagues as a vassal and also with the Fate of Geneva event chain. Burgundy often attacks Liege early in the game, drawing in Austria and its allies in a losing war. This can be a good opportunity for the player to declare war on Burgundy and take a couple of provinces. 
There are two main expanding routes: North Italy and Swabia (South Germany). Expanding into North Italy can be dangerous because of provinces with high development causing high Aggressive Expansion. Also, with the 1.30 patch, there is a chance for the Italian nations to stay in the HRE when the Shadow Kingdom event comes. But the player should expand there if there is an opportunity.

Swabia will be the best route to expand because if the player destroys all the countries with Swabian culture, Switzerland will get 15 army professionalism, 10 army tradition and permanent 25% mercenary manpower and 10% prestige from land battles modifiers.

#### The Protestant reformation[edit|edit source]

The player can't become the emperor of the HRE due to the government reform. There are some events for the reformed faith special to Switzerland (for example an event that spawns a reformed center of reformation in Geneva).

#### Idea Groups and Swiss Mercenaries[edit|edit source]

Switzerland can become a power house for mercenaries and military strength if the player wants to play it like that. In fact, Switzerland can become one of the most insanely powerful nations when it comes to mercenaries by the late game, with certain government reforms and the mechanics introduced to them in patch 1.35 with the Domination DLC.

To maximize the effectiveness of mercenaries, you need five idea groups - Economic, Infrastructure, Mercenary (naturally), Plutocratic, and Quality. Baseline with your national ideas, this gives you +10% Morale, +10% base Discipline, +30% Infantry Combat Ability, and another +10% Mercenary Discipline, with +80% to Mercenary Manpower, -45% Mercenary Cost, and -40% Mercenary Maintenance before calculating additional modifiers such as Army Professionalism, Policies, and government reforms. It is in combination with government reforms that things start to skyrocket. The Tier 1 Autonomous Swiss Cantons government reform grants you an additional +50% Mercenary Manpower, and the Swiss Mercenaries reform reduces mercenary cost by an additional 25% plus another +5% to Mercenary Discipline, while the Citizenry Electorate reform grants you another +10% Morale. With the Infrastructure-Mercenary policy active and maximized Mercenary Militarization along with the Economic-Quality policy active, you gain an additional +15% Mercenary Discipline, +5% Discipline, +25% Mercenary Manpower, and -10% Mercenary Maintenance. Additionally, completing the 'End the Hapsburgs' mission and Mercenary Monopoly event from Destroying the Swabians grants another +25% Mercenary Manpower and a +5% Morale boost. With a Discipline military advisor, this grants your mercenaries a modifier list of +25% Morale, +30% Infantry Combat Ability, +180% Mercenary Manpower, -70% Mercenary Cost, -50% Mercenary Maintenance, +20% Discipline, and then another +30% Mercenary Discipline on top of all that, regardless of your religion.

A viable substitution is the Defensive idea group for the Quality one, and the Religious idea group for Economic. With the Infrastructure-Mercenary and Religious-Mercenary policies and a Discipline advisor, you would have +50% Morale, +20% Infantry Combat Ability +10% Discipline, and +30% Mercenary Discipline, plus a Fort Defense of +60%. If you go Reformed, this maximizes you at a +60% Morale modifier with the War Fervor active, with around 130 to 140% Discipline on your mercenaries. #### Quick guide to reconquer Vaud, Valais and Geneva (from Savoy)[edit|edit source]

After having built many relations with countries such as the Papal States, Milan or some German states, you need to max out your army and potentially (if you have the Emperor DLC, if you hadn't acquired it yet and you possess 1 or more of stability) unlock "Consolidate Switzerland" in the mission tree. Once you have your maxed-out your army, you need to divide it in two: One bigger army, if not two medium sized armies to improve mobility (who need(s) a general with a good fire and shock), and a smaller army (to which you'd have to assign your general with the fastest maneuver). Before declaring war, position your troop as follows: The bulk of your army in the province of Fribourg/Freiburg, the smaller army in Waldstätten. The plan is to destroy the savoyard army as quickly as possible, so after declaring war your small army should move first and enter the only savoyard province that it can reach from Waldstätten, Valais. In peacetime the savoyard army is often positioned either at Chambery or Turin, so it will take time for it to move up. When it arrives, it will concentrate its forces at Valais to crush your small army, you count on that plan. By luring the savoyard army in Valais, you buy time for the bulk of your army to move to their pursuit and pin them in. Once it arrives, the battle may have already started, but the arrival of your army will seal the fate of the battle and potentially destroy the savoyard army completely. After this battle in Valais its just a long besieging campaign and hopefully you can even annex Savoy entirely and gain sea access.
