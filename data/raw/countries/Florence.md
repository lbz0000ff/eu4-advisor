### Strategy[edit|edit source]

#### Tuscany and Italy[edit|edit source]

As an Italian minor, the early game is not easy, and it can be wise to wait for Europe's geopolitical situation to stabilize before setting rivals. Yet,LuccaorSienarepresent easy targets early on, unless they join thetrade leagueof eitherGenoaorVenice.Milanusually is the only available relatively strong ally at game start and can be a good ally against the powerful merchant republics on either side of the Italian boot. If Milan is rivaling the Emperor of theHRE(usuallyAustria), an alliance with the emperor might be preferable as it usually forestalls the "Unlawful Territory" demand. Milan also has its own expansionist agenda, so eventually (especially if the player aims to form Italy) one must be ready to break their alliance and confront them in war. WhenNaplesbecomes independent later on, it can be a great ally againstThe Papal State.

Florence's mid-game goal is usually to formTuscanyand possiblyItaly. In order to form Tuscany, Florence must own its three starting provinces plus either Siena or Lucca. Vassalizing and then annexing them is not advisable as it requires more time. After Tuscany is formed, and theShadow Kingdom imperial incidenthas concluded, the player can expand to form Italy. Since Italian land gives a large amount of aggressive expansion from its high development, it can be a good idea to establish a foothold in North Africa, usually throughTunis, and expand there while European aggressive expansion cools down. Depending on the alliance situation after the Shadow Kingdom triggers, it can be good to ally nations that are rivals withAustriabut don't really want provinces in Italy, as it would allow the player to push north without competing with allies' interests.Franceis often the right choice; other options could includeCastile,Aragon, orHungary.

Florence/Tuscany is in the unique position of being able to strike at all the provinces needed to form Italy. With itsItalian Signoriagovernment, Florence is allowed to establish royal marriages with other Christian monarchies, as well as formingpersonal unions.

#### Colonization[edit|edit source]

The diplomatic strategy might require the early adoption ofDiplomatic,EspionageorInfluenceideas, which means sacrificing any real chance to colonization. But if chosen, the path of colonization can be chosen both early or late in the game. A good strategy could be to push through Egypt to gain access to the Red Sea and via that route to colonize Asia and Indochina, and push through to Australia. Alternatively, pushing through Morocco towards the rich gold provinces of West Africa is an optional strategy, although this route usually is more difficult because of the Reconquista by the Iberian monarchies. It could be good for the player to conquer parts of North Africa and get stronger with the help of colonies to then form Italy. If the player decides to do so, trade from Asia may be pulled from the Gulf of Aden trade node to Venice and from Timbuktu, trade may be pulled across the Sahara to Tunis and on to the Genoa trade node. Once Tuscany or Italy have become a great power later in the game, the player could choose to take over other nation's colonial nations to better expand its colonial domain.

#### Development[edit|edit source]

Florence is one of the strongest nations if a player is looking to play "tall" (i.e. stackingdevelopment costmodifiers to build up a small number of rich provinces). Florence starts as part of the HRE and the Italian provinces mostly consist of farmlands. Florence has access to the Genoa end trade node and multiple centers of trade nearby. Another bonus is that the Renaissance is guaranteed to spawn in Italy. Gaining control over that province provides the Birthplace of Renaissance modifier, lowering development cost by an extra5%. Being a republic also means that the player can spend Republican tradition to reelect its ruler and generate a good amount of monarch points.
