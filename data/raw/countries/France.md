Franceis akingdomlocated in WesternEurope, in the Franceregion, and could be one of the most (if not the most) interesting and entertaining nations to play in EUIV due to its powerful military and amount of flavor events. France allows for the player to make mistakes with usually limited consequences.  Due to its military and sheer size, taking down France as an outside nation can be difficult, but not impossible. In1444, it bordersBurgundyto the north and east,Englandto the north and west,Aragonto the south,Savoyto the east and a large number of minor nations all around. Notable areBrittany,Provence, thePapal States, andNavarra.

France starts out guaranteeing independence toScotland, in an alliance (as well as sharing dynasty) with Provence, and with cores on all the English land in France (excluding Calais).

### Missions[edit|edit source]

French missions are focused around uniting the decentralized French realm and further improving the economical power of the nation. The missions further the path of conquest of Italy through direct ownership of the land or through the use of the Golden March. France will also attempt to take control once again of theHoly Roman Empireto reclaim Charlemagne's inheritance. Dealing with England is also a major part of the mission tree and is fundamental if France is to rule the waves and become an important colonizer of the New and Old Worlds.

### Events[edit|edit source]

#### Wars of Religion[edit|edit source]

The rise ofProtestantism in Europe led to three decades of civil war within France known asFrench Wars of Religion. This disaster can occur in theAge of Reformation ifreligious unity is less than 75% and has Catholic, Protestant or Reformed provinces that aren't the state religion. Rival Catholic nations can form La Ligue Catholique, a major player in the French Wars of Religion, directed at the eradication of the Protestants in France. The League was used not only to defend the Catholic cause but also as a political tool in an attempt to usurp the French throne.

The formation of La Ligue Catholique will decrease relations with Catholic nations and give qualifying neighboring powers a casus belli to restore the one true faith!

#### Palace of Versailles[edit|edit source]

Around 1650, the player will be presented with the choice, through an event, to begin construction of the Palace of Versailles. A high initial cost to set the construction in motion, as well as a temporary but lengthy tax cut, can be a very costly procedure, where several loans might be required. However, finishing the Palace of Versailles will give France the desired model of kingly ruling, and as long as the Palace remains, France will benefit from yearly prestige and legitimacy. With theLeviathan DLC, theVersailles great projectis upgraded toSignificant without paying the usual cost or waiting for the usual construction time.

#### The French Revolution & Napoleon[edit|edit source]

Thisdisastercan occur after 1710 either if the French economy is on its knees with a high number of loans, is suffering from highwar exhaustion, has highabsolutism, hasrevolutionary ideasspread widely or is the most powerful nation in Europe. This disaster can create revolts, uprisings and even the overthrow of the monarchy! Sometime along this disaster, the player may get a 6/6/6 ruler named Napoléon Bonaparte.

### Strategy[edit|edit source]

Note:Some guides recommend to look at other startdates to trigger bugs which are favorable for France (they lose their T1 government reform and become an empire). This is not recommended, because it also causes many other bugs. Most of these are not immediately apparent and some of them can ruin a campaign later on.

#### Opening moves[edit|edit source]

France starts out with 3 discounted +2 advisors : Jacques Cœur (+2 adm, -10% stab cost), Charles d'Orléans (+2 adm, -0,10 yearly inflation) and Jean Jouvenel des Ursins (+2 dip, +20% improve relations). Taking 2 of these 3 is recommended, as France starts with a very decent economy that will be strengthened when its cores in Normandie and Gascogne are reclaimed. Since you will conquer quite a bit of land in the early years, giving out your crownland early by granting all +1 monarch point privileges at the start is not a bad idea.

Your first concern will be how to deal withEngland. They have fewer troops than France and are forced to navally invade, so defeating them in war and regaining the cores in Normandy and Aquitaine is not very difficult. France's thin coast means they start with a small navy and naval forcelimit, making naval invasion impossible. (This can be counteracted by getting military access throughScotlandbefore the war starts and invading England from the north.)

The easiest option is to wait for theSurrender of Maineevent to fire. The two options for the English AI are to hand Maine back, triggering a short truce. More likely it will refuse to do so, instead launching a offensive war against you. This will make for a easy war, because as the defender, you will be able to call in any allies, while England won't. If Scotland is called in, England will likely siege them down and ruin your war score.

A quicker alternative is to declare an offensive war before the Maine event fires, just a month after the start of the game. This will prevent you from calling in your allies, but with your armies and vassal swarm, the war is winnable against England and its usual allies ofPortugaland Irish minor(s). By taking the initiative in declaring the war, you make sure that the English AI does not pick the option to give you Maine in exchange for a 5-year-truce, which is not in your best interest as you want to get your cores back as soon as possible. By allyingBurgundyat the start (if it hasn't rivaled you), you might be able to call in Burgundy in the war by promising them land, but it will take much longer to get enough warscore to give Burgundy Calais, and if they have more participant score than that, they will lose trust. Often they will declare a separate war, which works out better.

Preparatory moves before this are to move your navy to your Atlantic ports. This allows you to blockade the fort when the English navy is not nearby and attack unescorted transports, hindering naval invasions. However your navy can not win a straight battle against the British and Portuguese navy, so avoid open battle and take opportunities as they arise. To aid this, use your higher siege pip General on the fort in Labourd, and using the other general on the Normandy fort. Portugal will get access throughCastile, so send more troops to guard against their attack on the fort, and get your vassals to attach to a stack nearby to reinforce, not the Normandy stack.

An alternate strategy might be to leave allies and vassals sieging England's French territories, while you send your armies in Scotland before the war to fight directly in Britain. This would allow you to earn greater warscore, and to take some British provinces from England, which will form your starting base for the next wars against the perfidious Albion.

Once the French forts are sieged down, head to Portugal. After being sieged down they can be offered a separate peace for gold, or if you are willing to commit to a long siege, their African fort in Ceuta (to fulfill theage objectivePresent on Two Continents). Eventually as ticking warscore raises, and England likely being hit with rebellions via theLollard HeresyandWar of the Roses, you will have enough warscore to take back all your cores. Additional objectives such as England's Irish province of The Pale or Calais may also be taken, but they take longer. Taking only your cores back fulfills initial missions to further claims and won't generate a coalition. Taking the province of The Pale would allow you to quickly expand into the British Isles through Ireland and Scotland, whose remoteness from mainland Europe will reduce aggressive expansion on the main powers of the continent. Eventually, an invasion of England from the North might be a much more secure path compared to an hazardous naval invasion.

Once you get your Norman cores back, you can fulfill the"Liberate Normandy"mission in yourmission treeto gain claims on allBrittanyfor your next round of expansion. They will usually get one or two allies ranging in effectiveness, from a powerful foe like Castile to nearby OPMs. Given their position away from other nations, they can be fully annexed without triggering a coalition.

Through the"Provençal Question"mission in yourmission tree, you can deal withProvencediplomatically by building up enoughtrustwith your ally (send a diplomat to curry favors in order to trade them for trust). The mission reward will make Provence your junior partner in apersonal union. While this option is preferable in terms of coalition management (as this gain of highly developed provinces won't generate any aggressive expansion), the number of free diplomatic relation slots you have is limited at the start of the game due to your important vassal swarm. As you won't be able to annex a junior partner until 50 years have passed, make sure that you don't need this diplomatic slot for stronger, more potent alliances. If you choose conquest instead, you can take advantage of the fact that Provence gets often excommunicated by the Papacy, allowing for the use of the excommunication CB, which greatly reduces aggressive expansion.

#### After England[edit|edit source]

From there, France has five common ways to expand. First, intoItaly. The French mission tree provides permanent claims in Italy, starting with Provence and moving on toSavoy,MilanandGenoa. This is a rich region and probably has the highest development in the game. However, it is a tightly contested region, and moving into it can make powerful enemies very quickly, especially the Holy Roman Emperor (usuallyAustria) if the player expands there before theShadow Kingdom imperial incidenthas triggered (usually in the 1460s) or if some of the targets have chosen to remain in the Empire. By the time the incident fires, you should have annexed some of your appanages, freeing diplo slots for diplomatic vassalisation of Italian minors. This will make conquest of the rest easier. Another good way to procede is by getting the"Transfer Subject" abilityin theAge of Discovery. It can be used to make other countries' subjects the player's subjects quicker and cheaper. It can be used to gain Naples from Aragon, which is probably most important since it is bigger than any other subjects on the Italian peninsula. Combined with some early conquests in Savoy, France can position itself to dominate Italy early.

Secondly, you can expand into theLow Countries. It is easier to expand into than Italy, as enemies in the region are usually very weak early (with the exception of Burgundy who shouldn't be taken on alone early), and there are usually few other great powers looking to expand into it that early. Like Italy, its provinces are rich and will help you control the trade node shared with England. An alternative route to conquering these provinces is to wait for theBurgundian inheritanceto fire. To trigger this event, the player must have initiated a royal marriage with Burgundy. If Burgundy has rivaled you first, you can ask them to cancel their rivalry in a peace deal. Then, if you improve relations with them, they will accept an alliance and a royal marriage. Nonetheless, the player needs to be aware of theDutch revoltsthat happen between 1550 and 1650 with large and recurring rebel armies. This can be avoided by either changing the culture of conquered Dutch provinces to Francien before 1550 or waiting with conquering the territories until after 1650. Alternatively, you can move your capital to the Low Countries region, but that only works as long as theNetherlandsdon't form by revolting from another country. Moving your capital into the Low Countries has also the benefit of moving your trading port to the usually richer English Channel node.

Thirdly, you can expand intoBritainand England. Though unorthodox, taking English forts instead of your cores in the Maine war is a viable strategy. Expanding this way lends you relatively good provinces with the benefit that these provinces will never be touched bydevastationonce the conquest is complete. Should you go this way, it is advised to take the forts of London, Northumbria, and Calais in the Maine war (take as many of your cores as you can of course, focusing on forts and provinces needed for missions). With this move, you have shortened all future wars with England by stripping them of their fortifications, as well as making the English navy completely useless. From here, England can be mopped up fairly quickly (assuming they don't get powerful allies). When taking provinces, focus on taking the forts in Glamorgan and Norfolk as well as the trade centers of Gloucester and York. ReleasingNorthumberlandisn't a bad idea, as they have many cores that can be reconquered.

Fourth, one can expand south, intoIberiaand thenAfrica. Historically France invaded Algeria, and it is an easy area to move into if you take the Baleares or Sardinia fromAragon, or Ceuta from Portugal during a war with England. There is usually very little competition for the area with other great powers (except Castile/Spain), and the countries in the area are usually very weak, especiallyTlemcen. However, the area is Muslim, making it harder to convert and worsening rebellions. The region also has weak development, meaning that the gains made from it are smaller than in Italy or the Low Countries, but it is easier to take and maintain. If you're doing this, Religious or Humanist ideas are a must early on (though Religious is recommended for long-term benefits of actually converting provinces).

The player may find an attack ofAragonprofitable if theIberian Weddinghasn't fired (yet) with the help ofCastileto take the Pyrenees, the Balearics, Sardinia or Sicily (to gain an access to North Africa), as it is an important and easy land to conquer, but also to consolidate French control over the Genoa trade node. During the peace deal, forcing Aragon to releaseNaplesas a sovereign state or even transfer it to France if possible will weaken both Aragon andSpainin future if it forms, and make conquest in the region easier in the future. It is equally possible to use the Balearics to releaseCataloniaand use its cores on the Spanish Mediterranean coast to greatly mitigate aggressive expansion in the area. However, trying to get apersonal unionon Castile or Spain through royal marriage might be preferable than antagonizing them, as it will allow you to get for yourself all their colonies in the New World once you have integrated them.

The last path is colonialism. England, Spain, and Portugal will have better colonial expansion benefits through better settler increase, more colonists through national ideas, better control over the relevant trade nodes, and different colonial events. However, France's fourth national idea grants a−50%bonus tonative uprising chance, which stacks perfectly with the same bonus from theNative Trading Policy. It will give France no native attacks and a very good assimilation rate (giving a bonus to goods produced). This makes France among the best colonial nations without the need for moving troops into the New World. France also has some unique historical colonialeventsandmissions. Be aware, however, that in multiplayer France will often have strong enemies in the mainland, and oftentimes expansion might not be a good choice for the French player.

After this, the game is completely open to you. If you are doing an active colonial game, France's mighty military will allow you to obtain some achievements that another colonial power would get with difficulty, likeMaster of India,Trade Hegemon, orThe Rising Sun. If you stay in Europe you will likely concentrate on the Holy Roman Empire and Austria. You can expand everywhere, the possibilities are limitless.

#### Religion and religious wars[edit|edit source]

During the Reformation, France gets a specialdisaster, theFrench Wars of Religion. This disaster is similar to theReligious Turmoil disaster, but with different events and trigger conditions (see its article for details). Because this disaster has very high chances of happening, you may choose to stayCatholic or goProtestant (orReformed) and both options will have the same consequences. Keep in mind though that going Protestant will remove theTreaty of Tordesillasfrom your colonies, but will give better bonuses than the papacy bonuses like colonial growth, morale, discipline, or a reduced development cost. If you join theProtestant leagueyou can try todismantlethe Holy Roman Empire during the league war if the Emperor and all the Electors are involved. This will remove the+50%aggressive expansion penalty of the HRE, so coalitions will be less problematic, and you will be able to easily conquer the German minors now that no Emperor will defend them.

#### Quick Roman Empire[edit|edit source]

France is one of the strongest nations for a Quick Roman empire, although it's a bit more prone to fail to RNG than nations likeAragon. France can attackByzantiumwithout a Casus belli on 11/12/1444. If this strategy is picked, naval invasion should be handled with care, as France's initial 2 heavies + 8 cogs are a bit less strong than the Byzantine navy. It's advised to ask theOttomansfor military access, in this case. Most of the time, in 1.35, the Ottomans start at -19 relations or so, and are not hostile, so a few months of improving relations to get them to positive will do the trick. In the month before the war, you should ally many nations and not be afraid of going over your relation slots, as it will be very helpful in the war. Ideally, you want about 4 ofBohemia,Aragon,Castile,Poland,Hungary,Venice,Burgundy(it seems like Burgundy starts always either Hostile or Rival in 1.35, so it's very unlikely) and other decent options areThe Papal State,SavoyandFlorence. It's not recommended to allyMilan, as the event « The Milanese succession » gives a Restoration of Union casus belli for only 5 years and it's likely to fire (it fires if Filipo Maria Visconti dies and Milan chooses the Ambrosian Republic).

If going for a quick Roman empire, picking Jean Jouvenel des Ursins (discounted +2 diplomat) and Jacques Cœur (discounted +2 artist) as advisors is a great option, as Cœur will reduce the cost of increasing stability back to 0 or 1 after the no-CB war, and des Ursins will make the aggressive expansion decay faster.

Some care must be taken to make sure that the Ottomans actually declare war on Byzantium, but that the Ottomans don't get too many occupation to prevent the player from vassalizing Byzantium.

After vassalizing Byzantium and taking Achaïe, Corinthe and Athènes (it's impossible to take Athens alone, as it's barely outside of coring range, and vassalizing Athens is pointless), the player will be dragged into a defensive war, where all the Appanages and allies will be able to participate. The player should keep an eye on theOttomansmilitary technology, as having a 6/4/6 starting ruler means that they are very likely to unlock mil tech 4 before you and your allies. In this case, just retreat, dance around their armies, and occupy their provinces when their armies are not there to impoverish them. When tech 4 is unlocked, you can engage in battles again.

Whenever Burgundy flips friendly, if the Burgundian inheritance has not fired, ally them immediately, then send them a Royal Marriage offer. This will make you eligible for the Burgundian Inheritance, giving a lot of land for free. To avoid the Dutch revolts, it's possible to move the capital to a province like Bruges, which is in the Low Countries but not in the HRE.

An event that grants a Subjugation casus belli onNaplesfires very often, and it's recommended to subjugate, as it will incur a lot less aggressive expansion than conquering directly, and Naples is a pretty useful vassal.

#### Recommended idea groups[edit|edit source]

- EitherDiplomaticorEspionage: This choice will allow better relations with neighbors and to avoid coalitions, which is critical when dealing with Western Europe high development provinces or the HRE. The reduced aggressive expansion via having extradiplomats improving relations and theimprove relations modifier, the reducedaggressive expansion impact or the reducedprovince warscore cost are very valuable.
- Influence: As many opportunities arise through the game to gain junior partners inpersonal unions, Influence allow you to make good use of them and reduce theirannexation cost.
- Exploration: You might find the need to colonize a little bit, since building the French army up to the maximum force limit and fully maintaining it provokes a mid-sized deficit without seeking additional sources of income. Colonizing the Caribbean and Gulf of Saint Laurence trade nodes will fix the economic problem (because these trade nodes are directly connected to the Bordeaux trade node). If you managed to conquer the British Islands and inherited or conquered the Dutch lands belonging to Burgundy, you may colonize the Chesapeake trade node, connected to the English Channel trade node, and collect the trade there. Colonizing Africa for later access to Indian trade can also be lucrative.
- Expansion: This allows faster colonization if you're playing an active colonial game, and can thus help to make more money from tariffs or trade companies if theWealth of NationsDLC is enabled.
- Administrative: This is an essential idea group for a world conquest because of the extragoverning capacity and reducedcore creation cost.
- EitherOffensiveorDefensive: Offensive provides a very strong +20%siege ability, and the general pips combined with high army tradition makes it very likely to get 3-star generals. In the late game, adding Force limit is always useful, too. Defensive, on the other side, provides cheaper armies and defensive, combined with the second national ideaÉlan!, will provide a huge bonus of +35%morale of armies. Thus, France is perhaps the easiest nation to get the "My armies are invincible!"achievement. Defensive doesn't shorten sieges, unlike Offensive, but it reduces losses due to attrition, letting you fight wars for longer.
- Quality: Quality is far inferior to other military groups by itself, but it provides very interesting policies. It should not be taken if the goal is purely to win battles, as the best value in this case would be Defensive ideas. The best policy among these with Quality is Quality-Influence, giving another -10% diplomatic annexation cost, on top of Influence ideas giving -25%, and the Vassal integration act (Influence-Administrative) giving another -15%. This combines well with either the Crusader Kings path missions, giving France avery powerful vassalinThe Papal States, or with integrating a Personal unions obtained either through missions or royal mariages.
- Quantity: More troops are always useful. This combined with Quality or Defensive will make the French army almost unstoppable. This can also be very helpful if you are facing a coalition war and have to send troops to all fronts, and of course, more troops mean more units to garrison the New World and Asian colonies.
Keep in mind that this is completely optional, and this can change depending on your game. Example: If you're working on conquering Muslims in North Africa, it might be more useful to take religious or humanist ideas instead of exploration or quantity.

#### Optional early moves[edit|edit source]

- You can try to vassalizeNavarraearly on. An improve relations advisor and diplomatic interactions may help here. Great power influence, guarantees, allowing mil access, and gifts can all make sure you get the vassalization before they fall into the Aragon PU by event.
- Focus on yourmissiontargets. Permanent claims reduce a lot of admin for coring and diplo (since you avoid unjustified demands), meaning you have more to stay on time in tech or expand further.
- Going into Iberia generates less aggressive expansion. You can rush into the Berbers which generate almost no AE for Catholics. Make sure to try to get the Tafilalt gold mine in Morocco and state it for maximum income. The La Mancha gold mine is also a serious target. Devving gold mines to about 8 or so can really help with supplementing the already large French income.
- The best way to get into Britain is to take one province in Ireland from the peace deal and attack from there with almost all your armies in the next war. Alternatively, you can attempt to vassalize an Irish minor, such asDesmond, for easy access. Lastly, as long as you call Scotland (which you can ally day 1) into war with England, none of your troops in their land will be in exile, letting you keep the majority of your army in Britain to fight English forces on their own soil.
- Italy is abnormally rich. Taking the entire Genoa trade node and making it your centre of trade can generate a lot of income. Take care about aggressive expansion though. Postponing until the Shadow Kingdomimperial incidentfires is advised.
