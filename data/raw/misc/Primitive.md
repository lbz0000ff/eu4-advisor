### Primitives[edit|edit source]

To determine if a nation should be primitive, the game does the following check:

One of the following must be true:

- Is a Native tribe
- All of the following must be true:Religion is Inti, Maya, or NahuatlHas never reformed their religionBecame Inti, Maya, or Nahuatl while having a primitive tech group
- Religion is Inti, Maya, or Nahuatl
- Has never reformed their religion
- Became Inti, Maya, or Nahuatl while having a primitive tech group
The game classifies the following Tech groups as "Primitive":

- North American
- Mesoamerican
- Andean
- South American
- Aboriginal
- Polynesian
Should a nation become a primitive religion while not being in a primitive tech group, they will count as having reformed their religion for the rest of the game.

Effectively this means that only nations starting in Australia and the Americas should ever be primitive. However a Polynesian nation would become Primitive if they reform to one of the primitive religions. Also, nations that are released by Native Tribes will also be Native Tribes and thus will be Primitive.

### Effect of primitivity[edit|edit source]

Effects of being primitive:

- -90%income fromgold
- +50%development cost
- Can only buildtransport ships
- Naval andMaritime ideas are disabled
- Cannotforce religion on subjects via subject interactions
Effects for nations interacting with primitives :

- Conquering provinces from a primitive as a non-primitive doesn't cost unjustified demands
- Conquering provinces from a primitive as a non-primitive doesn't cause separatism
- Overseas provinces with a core from a existing primitive country get−80%culture conversion cost
- An army led by aconquistadorcan move through the land of primitives without need for access. This also applies to the conquistadors of other primitive nations.
- Armies led by conquistadors wont be exiled while they are inside a primitive nation that war is declared upon. This also applies to the conquistadors of other primitive nations.
Additionally, although not tied directly to Primitivity, all primitive nations are unable to manually develop institutions.

### Types of primitives[edit|edit source]

Broadly speaking, primitive nations can be split into two different groups, based on how their reform process works (see below). This section will briefly discuss unique mechanics and attributes these groups have in addition to the shared effects of being primitive.

#### Native tribes[edit|edit source]

This group makes up all nations in North and South America, as well as Australia except for nations that are Inti, Mayan, or Nahuatl.

Native tribes have a gameplay loop that is completely different from normal nations. To see a in depth explanation of all the effects their T0 "Native Tribe" government gives them, check out their main page (link above).

The big difference is that these nations have "Tribal Land" instead of "regular" provinces. These provinces can potentially migrate, have access to Native buildings instead of normal buildings and cant manually spawn institutions.

#### Religious primitives[edit|edit source]

This group makes up all nations in North and South America that are Inti, Mayan, or Nahuatl.

Religious Primitives do not have access to the "Government reforms" interface. They also don't passively accumulate reform progress, can however gain some from one time increases, such as events. Even thoough the "Government reforms" button is greyed out, should a primitive somehow be able to gain enough reformprogress this way, the "you can enact a new government reform" banner will grant access to the "Government reforms" interface. In any case, these nations still start with a T1 reform enacted from which they can benefit.

### Reforming into a non-primitive nation[edit|edit source]

To lose the handicaps that primitivity puts on a nation, they will have to reform their nation.

#### Inti, Mayan and Nahuatl nations[edit|edit source]

The Mayan, Inti and Nahuatl religions each have a unique mechanic to enact five religious reforms. The interface for these can be found at the bottom right of the screen. Enacting these reforms requires unique conditions for each of the three religions (see below). The reforms all offer permanent bonuses at a cost that depends on the religion (see below). The reforms can be passed in any order. After all 5 have been passed, the "Reform Religion" button becomes enabled.

Reforming the religion requires a core province with a direct land border with a core province of a neighbor that fulfills the following conditions:

- Has embraced any institution
- Does not follow the Inti, Mayan or Nahuatl faith
Reforming the religion grants the nation a technology boost (which brings it up to 75% of the neighboring nation’s technology level in each category). It also immediately grants all theinstitutions that the bordering province had, fully spreading them to all owned provinces. Furthermore you gain all government reforms of the nation that you reform of, minus their last two tiers. So if you reform of a nation that has 6 tiers of reform enacted, you will gain all their reforms up to tier 4. Should the nation have enacted a reform you cannot enact, you will be able to select another reform at that tier, for no cost. You don't actually gain any reform progress from reforming.

Lastly and most importantly, if the nation that was reformed off of was not primitive, the reforming nation will get rid of theirprimitivestatus.

##### Inti[edit|edit source]

An Inti state that fulfills the following conditions can enact a religious reform:

- Is at peace
- Has positivestability
- Has no rebel-controlled provinces
- Has 100 authority
- Owns at least 10provinces
Doing so will reduce stability by−2, remove all their authority and spark a civil war as a pretender exploits the loss of authority to attempt to seize the throne for themselves. This rebellion will always consist of two pretender stacks who will usually be 9k and a 12k stack. If these rebels enforce their demands, two religious reforms are lost, greatly setting back the country's progress towards reforming the religion. If theInca"The Solar Cult" mission was completed, stability will only be reduced by−1and Authority will be set to20.

The Inti faith is about maintaining the authority of the Sapa Inca by having the people worship him as a god. Inti nations have an authority value that goes up from owning vast stretches of territory, and goes down when the autonomy of a province the country owns increases (either from manually increasing it, being forced to by rebels, or choosing to do so in an event). Authority ranges between 0 and 100. It is also affected by a number ofunique eventsfor the Inti religion.
Authority is gained and lost as follows:

- Yearly authority:+0.02timesautonomy-modified development
- Yearly authority:+0.15with Religious idea 4: Devoutness
- Yearly authority: between−0.25and+0.25fromdevotion(as atheocracy)
- Yearly authority:+0.1/+0.2/+0.3from//of the monumentsChan Chan Citadel,Qhapaq Ñan, andTiwanaku
- Authority from autonomy:−0.2or+0.2per point of autonomy raised or lowered respectively. This includes autonomy changes from events and rebels. The authority gain is calculated without considering that the actual autonomy change might be different if the province reaches its minimum or maximum autonomy.
The standard action of raising autonomy in a province will raise autonomy by 25 and will give−5authority, while lowering autonomy by 25 will give+5authority.

##### Nahuatl[edit|edit source]

Nahuatl nations need to fulfill the following conditions to enact a reform :

- Stability is positive
- Has no rebel-controlled provinces
- Is at peace
- Has at least 5 subjects
- Doom is less than 25
When enacting a reform, Doom will increase by 50,stability drops by−1, and all subject will gain+40%Liberty desire. Disloyal Subjects will break free.. Furthermore, each reform decreases the rate at which the country accumulates Doom by 20% from the base rate. Enacting all five reforms effectively stops Doom from increasing every year, although it can still increase through special events.

Each Nahuatl state has a ticking Doom value. Should the value ever reach 100 the Nahuatl state will be forced into taking drastic measures to avert Doomsday. The ruling family will be sacrificed, killing the ruling monarch and heir and replacing them with a 0/0/0 ruler. In addition, all of the country's monarch power is lost and any and all subject states break away. If the state had already enacted any religious reforms, up to two of these will be lost. The Doom mechanic is removed when the 5th and final reform is passed. Doom is also affected by a number ofunique eventsfor the Nahuatl religion.
To avert Doomsday, Nahuatl states have a few options. The "Flower Wars" casus belli gives them the ability to declare war on their neighbours freely. Occupying provinces and fighting battles will result in Doom being reduced as they secure captives to send to the gods. If just warring with neighbours isn't sufficient, a Nahuatl state can also sacrifice ruling monarchs and adult heirs in their vassal states. Doing so will reduce Doom by an amount equal to the total skills of that monarch or heir, but will anger all subject states and make them more likely to seek independence. There is a 3-year cooldown before you can sacrifice another ruler/heir from any country. Nahuatl countries can declare war during regency.

Doom is gained and lost as follows:

### General Info and Strategy[edit|edit source]

Since Primitives cannot easily embrace institutions, this will slow their technological advancement and thus, they will be technologically inferior to most other non-tribal nations, most notably the European powers.

This means that a Primitives relative position to Europe can dictate how difficult a specific nation will be to play.

Primitives situated on the west coast of the Americas will be ignored by European powers for longer and are thus safe from aggression, placing them in a better position initially but this does have the unfortunate drawback of having to wait longer to reform their governments (if in the Americas) or having a difficult time embracing newinstitutions. In addition to that, once the Europeans do reach these areas, they will be even more technologically advanced and even if the primitive can reform their government, there likely will not be much, if any, uncolonized land left to expand into, placing them in a bad situation even after reforming their government.

Conversely, primitives situated on the east coast of the Americas are in a much more dangerous position initially as they will be right on the front lines of European colonization once the European powers arrive and will thus bear the brunt of European aggression. This does mean, however, that if the aforementioned primitives are able to survive the initial contact, they will be in a much better position to reform their government, embrace institutions and begin expanding themselves, perhaps even carving out an empire of their own.

During a normal game, most (if not all) primitives will eventually be overpowered and annexed by the stronger European nations, but if a skilled player takes control of a primitive, they can make an attempt to survive the initial contact, reform the government and beat back and expel the foreign invaders from the continent altogether.

Since reforming the government will turn you into the same type of government as the nation that you reform off, you will usually turn into a Republic, as all Colonial Nations are Republics. If this is undesired it is advised to quickly expand towards the coast so that a Coloniser can be bordered before they own 5 provinces in your colonial region.

If a european power declares war on you before you are ready to defend yourself, it is often easier to just give up some of your Land. As all overseas Land will be given to Colonial nations, it is very easy to take it back later. As long as your Capital stays in the new world, the Colony's overlord wont be called into war against you. The AI does tend to enforce peace tho, so its advised to rush the Colony down. Its also worth considering to still keep the Colonial nation alive in one province, because as long as it exists, all Land that is colonized in the region will continue to be given to them, avoiding a situation where for example Portugal owns 4 provinces in the region, forcing you to go to war against them directly if you want those provinces.

If war is inevitable and surrender isn't an option, Nations that are located in Mexico or Peru have the benefit of extremely defensible Terrain. Since most of your nation is made up of mountains, invaders will take much attrition while laying siege, and will always be suffering a huge combat penalty, if the right engagements are taken. Furthermore, wannabe Colonizers will be forced to slowly send over their armies to the new world either via naval landing, or from a small colonial base on your shores. This slow trickle of armies allows a perceptive player to wipe army after army before they can link up, resulting in horrible losses for the Invader. It should be noted however, that as the game progresses and the Colonizers grab more and more Land in the new world, it will be easier for them to stage an invasion. In the late game even the armies of their colonial nations can become a problem by sheer numbers alone.

Even though Natives receive only 10% of the Gold income, one should not shy away from developing their gold provinces. Native lands are often so rich in gold, that even with only 10% of the income, your nation should be able to be financed mostly by gold.

#### European Diseases[edit|edit source]

Nations with their capital in north or south america that havenotembraced Feudalism are liable to trigger the "European Diseases" events. These events will (in addition to their devastating effects) enable the "Rapid collapse of society" triggered modifier, granting :

- +33%All power costs
- −10%Discipline
- −20%Land morale
It is thushighlyadvised to get Feudalism as quickly as possible.
