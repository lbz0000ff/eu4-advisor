Random NationsorRandom Setupis a setting changeable atgame set-up. It shuffles nearly all country tags randomly on the map, assigning them religion and culture based on where their new capital is located. There are two random setup settings  - Random NationsDynamicorHistorical.Achievementscan not be earned with this option.

### General Properties[edit|edit source]

A game with random setup on can only be started if the player creates a custom nation. To see a fully random world, it is recommended to create your custom nation on a faraway island and then, if possible, remove it - examples include South Georgia, Hollhavai and St. Helena island, so your country would impact the algorithms as little as possible. 
While the way countries are randomized exactly is unknown, generally the game attempts to make an equal amount of great powers, medium powers and small powers to vanilla, with roughly similar sizes as vanilla. Nonetheless, region, religion, and culture distribution of the powers is fully random, and the noise of these powers differs from generation to generation greatly. One game might have multiple 350 development nations, and another might have 250 as the maximum.

Religionandculturesof Random Setup are identical to the base game.

While generally the game attempts to not create countries in uncolonized territories, it still might, especially present in the Pacific Ocean, Siberia, South America and Africa. 
No government reforms, claims and cores, or starting diplomacy are set, with the exception of North and South American as well as Australian nativemigratory tribesbeing set to so.HREandEmperor Of Chinamechanics still exist. The country owning Nanjing is more likely to be theemperor; nations in theHoly Roman Empireare more likely to be small and theemperor/electorroles are given randomly.

### Random Nations[edit|edit source]

##### Historical Random Nations[edit|edit source]

Historicalkeeps the flags and colors of countries the same, otherwise it is no different from Dynamic random nations -There are still no intended country-specific mission trees and no formable nations (except forMamluksbut a player might need to conquer the original country first). It is better known than dynamic random nations.

theDynamicoption assigns the countries random flags and national ideas akin to the native nations of theRandom New World(The country's color remains the same) and removes all tag-switching formables (exceptMamluks) only keeping the "FormCaliphate" decision available and removing all non-generic mission trees (Mission trees added inWinds Of ChangeDLC andLeviathanDLC miss a trigger and remain).

Other gameplay elements like ai weights foridea groupsare also unchanged - For example, the country with POR will still nearly always pick exploration ideas if possible, even if it is a country in india and not Portugal.

Dynamic random nations borrow their names from the names of their capital provinces if small country, area if medium sized country, and region if large sizes country. How dynamic nations call themselves after said regions can be found andmodifiedin

### Modding[edit|edit source]

Similarly toRandom New World, while the distribution and generation of countries and ideas might not be changeable, it is still possible to improve the gameplay capabilities of the feature by adding events, scripted effects, missions and decisions that are based on arbitrary things like culture, and not country tags.
